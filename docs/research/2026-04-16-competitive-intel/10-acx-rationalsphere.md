# ACX / Rationalsphere as Distribution Channel for Argumend

*Research date: 2026-04-16*

## 1. Snapshot

**Astral Codex Ten** (ACX, astralcodexten.substack.com) is Scott Alexander's Substack, the successor to *Slate Star Codex* (SSC, 2013-2020). It is arguably the single largest epistemics-adjacent newsletter on the internet. Substack ranks ACX #2 in the Science category. Exact subscriber counts are not public, but in the 2024 subscription drive Scott disclosed that ACX "gains about 20,000 unpaid subscribers per year" while losing ~500 paid annually — a ~10% paid churn rate that he explicitly tries to offset each year ([Subscrive Drive 2024](https://www.astralcodexten.com/p/subscrive-drive-2024-free-unlocked)). Given five years at ~20k/year plus carryover from SSC, a total free list of well north of 100k is *likely*, and paid subscribers are in the low-to-mid five figures (unclear — Scott only reveals trend data, not totals). ACX posts routinely clear 500+ comments; the February 2026 Links post shipped 62 curated items ([Links For February 2026](https://www.astralcodexten.com/p/links-for-february-2026)).

**r/slatestarcodex** sits at ~63k members (the subreddit has been quarantined, which throttles third-party stats trackers) ([subredditstats](https://subredditstats.com/r/slatestarcodex)). It is the community hub for sober, non-political discussion of ACX content, with sister subreddits r/themotte (heated politics, now off-Reddit) and a large Discord.

**Adjacent infrastructure:**
- **ACX Grants** funded 42 of 654 applicants in 2025 ($5k-$150k range; next round likely late 2026/early 2027) ([ACX Grants Results 2025](https://www.astralcodexten.com/p/acx-grants-results-2025)).
- **ACX Meetups Everywhere Spring 2026** scheduled events in *over 180 cities* worldwide ([Meetups Everywhere Spring 2026](https://www.astralcodexten.com/p/meetups-everywhere-spring-2026-times)).
- **LessWrong** curated mailing list: 30k+ subscribers, ~50% open rate ([LessWrong feature post](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/5rygaBBH7B4LNqQkz/new-feature-your-subscribed-feed)).
- **Zvi Mowshowitz** (*Don't Worry About the Vase*): 33k+ subscribers, Substack #94 in Technology ([Sidestack listing](https://sidestack.io/directory/substack/thezvi)).

## 2. Product vs. Argumend

ACX is not a product competitor — it's a distribution channel and a content benchmark. But there is a critical conceptual overlap: Scott's *"X: Much More Than You Wanted To Know"* series ("Ivermectin: MMTYWTK", "Prison and Crime: MMTYWTK", "Face Masks: MMTYWTK") is essentially prose-form argument mapping. He lays out positions, arrays evidence, identifies cruxes, and adjudicates. These posts routinely go viral because the rationalsphere reads them as *the* authoritative synthesis ([example: Ivermectin MMTYWTK on LessWrong](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/3iMXHJCWNbmxr9peT/scott-alexander-s-ivermectin-much-more-than-you-wanted-to)).

The threat: Scott's readers already have a trusted human synthesizer. They don't obviously need a tool. The opportunity: a LessWrong post titled *"Why is 'Argument Mapping' Not More Common in EA/Rationality?"* explicitly names this gap — argument mapping *sounds* like it should be rationalist canon, yet adoption is near-zero, and the top objection cited is that existing tools (Kialo especially) produce *worse* reading experiences than well-written prose ([LW post](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/bHXpbf6jXc4bgyrrp/why-is-argument-mapping-not-more-common-in-ea-rationality)). This is Argumend's exact positioning question. Rationalists *do* argument mapping under the label "double crux" but refuse tool-based versions. If Argumend produces an MMTYWTK-grade *artifact* — not a generic tool — it lands.

## 3. Acquisition story

SSC grew by being linked-to-reflexively. Scott writes extremely long, extremely rigorous, often-contrarian analyses that function as terminal citations: once someone reads "I Can Tolerate Anything Except The Outgroup" or "Meditations On Moloch", they link it forever because no one else has written the canonical treatment. The viral loop: (1) a post lands, (2) Tyler Cowen / Marginal Revolution / Hacker News front-page picks it up, (3) it becomes the reference everyone cites in arguments for ~5 years. The 2020 NYT doxxing incident *accelerated* growth — Scott deleted the blog in protest, drove a Streisand-effect wave of press coverage (National Review, Reason, The Daily Beast, New Statesman all wrote defenses), and then launched ACX on Substack January 2021 with the inherited goodwill and a paid tier ([Wikipedia: Slate Star Codex](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Slate_Star_Codex), [Reason](https://reason.com/2020/06/24/slate-star-codex-dox-scott-alexander-new-york-times/)).

The 2017 Rethink Charity EA survey ranked SSC as the #4 way people first discovered Effective Altruism — a distribution moat no new blog will replicate.

## 4. Plateau or death cause

ACX is not dying — it's mature and constrained. Constraints:

- **Scott deliberately suppresses subscribe prompts.** He asked Substack to remove standard upsells; consequence: "people don't think about subscribing and I keep losing subscribers" ([Subscrive Drive 2024](https://www.astralcodexten.com/p/subscrive-drive-2024-free-unlocked)).
- **Post cadence** is ~3-5 posts/week split across long essays, Open Threads, Hidden Open Threads (paid), Links roundups, Classifieds, and book review contest entries. Scott is one person.
- **No explicit editorial sponsor policy on the About page** ([ACX About](https://www.astralcodexten.com/about)). Promotion happens via (a) Links, (b) Classifieds threads, (c) explicit favors Scott owes, (d) ACX Grants funding, or (e) guest posts won via the Book Review / Non-Book Review contests. Commercial paid placement does not appear to exist.
- **Substack saturation**: Scott notes "everyone I know reads a sample of the same set of Substacks — mine, Matt Yglesias', Freddie de Boer's, Stuart Ritchie's" ([Universe-Hopping Through Substack](https://www.astralcodexten.com/p/universe-hopping-through-substack)). Adjacency is a small, overlapping set.

  - Non-Book Review Contest 2025 winners Scott has formally cross-recommended (King of Daydreams, Everest Era, Canis Agrippae, Journal Club with Myka, Mistakes Were Made, and 5 others) ([ACX Recommendations](https://www.astralcodexten.com/recommendations))

## 6. Kill-shots for Argumend

Ranked by expected ROI. *Be specific.*

**HIGH — Publish an MMTYWTK-grade artifact using Argumend.**
The single highest-leverage move. Pick one topic (e.g., "Lab Leak: Argument Map Much More Than You Wanted To Know", "Ivermectin: Re-Mapped", or something fresh like "GLP-1s And Alzheimer's"), use Argumend to produce a visual artifact + 4,000-word written companion, publish it on a standalone URL, cross-post on LessWrong and EA Forum, and tweet at @slatestarcodex, @thezvi, @dwarkesh_sp. Scott links *at least 50 external things per month* in Links roundups and the format specifically rewards "novel synthesis on a contested empirical question" ([Links Feb 2026](https://www.astralcodexten.com/p/links-for-february-2026)). Don't pitch the tool — pitch the *output*. Getting one item into a Links roundup is worth ~20k click-throughs.

**HIGH — Apply for ACX Grants when next round opens.**
The 2025 round funded 42/654 applicants including *prediction-market interfaces, data-fabrication detection software, AI introspection training, automated forecasting* — i.e., epistemics tooling is explicitly in-scope ([ACX Grants Results 2025](https://www.astralcodexten.com/p/acx-grants-results-2025)). Typical grant: $25-50k. Next round likely opens late 2026 or early 2027. Application form takes 15-30 min. Argumend fits the stated thesis ("novel approaches to systemic change… new social institutions") nearly perfectly. Even if rejected, being listed in the applications pool means Manifund contributors see it.

**HIGH — Enter the 2026 Non-Book Review window or 2027 Book Review contest.**
2026 is a Book Review year (deadline May 20, 2026 — ~5 weeks away as of this report). Finalists and winners get (1) free ACX subscriptions, (2) sidebar links, (3) *right to pitch guest posts*, and (4) their top-of-post bio linked publicly ([Book Review Contest Rules 2026](https://www.astralcodexten.com/p/book-review-contest-rules-2026)). A book review *produced with visible support from Argumend's analysis engine* (e.g., "argument map of the book's central claim" embedded) is a direct distribution wedge. The 2025 Non-Book Review winner Edward Nevraumont turned his placement into an ongoing Substack.

**MEDIUM — ACX Classifieds "Consume My Product/Service" post.**
Scott runs Classifieds threads on an irregular schedule (latest: 4/25) with categories including *Consume My Product/Service* and *Read My Blog* ([ACX Classifieds 4/25](https://www.astralcodexten.com/p/acx-classifieds-425)). A single top-level comment costs nothing, reaches ~hundreds of thousands of pageviews, and is on-pattern. Downside: conversion is modest because readers skim. Use as a consistent secondary channel, not primary.

**MEDIUM — Sponsor or speak at ACX Meetups (Berkeley, NYC, London, Boston).**
With 180+ cities in Spring 2026, direct in-person demos at the Berkeley (Tuesday after Less.Online, 2740 Telegraph Ave) or Boston/NYC meetups put Argumend in front of the densest concentration of rationalists anywhere. Founder attendance costs only travel. Scott personally appears at Berkeley. No formal sponsorship channel is advertised, but offering to cover pizza + a 5-minute demo to a local organizer is within norms.

**MEDIUM — Pitch Zvi Mowshowitz directly.**
Zvi aggregates far more than Scott does (multiple weekly roundups on AI, covid, games) with 33k+ subscribers. He has a much higher link-density per post and actively seeks epistemics tools. An Argumend-produced "AI 2027 scenario mapped as an argument graph" would plausibly be reshared by Zvi given his prior AI 2027 coverage ([Zvi on AI 2027](https://thezvi.substack.com/p/ai-2027-dwarkeshs-podcast-with-daniel)).

**MEDIUM — Cross-post flagship analyses to LessWrong.**
LW has 30k+ mailing list subs with ~50% open rate. A well-tagged LW post with an Argumend output embedded gets curator attention. Tag: "Epistemics", "Rationality".

**LOW — Pitch Dwarkesh Patel for podcast.**
Very high-value if it lands, but Dwarkesh interviews recognizable names (Scott, Dario, Demis, Zuck). Not a realistic first move; becomes possible *after* a viral MMTYWTK artifact.

**LOW — Cold-email Scott.**
Contact is scott[at]slatestarcodex[dot]com ([ACX About](https://www.astralcodexten.com/about)). Scott reads pitches but rarely responds; he prefers to discover things in his RSS / Twitter loop. Don't make this the primary play — seed Twitter/LessWrong/EA Forum first so the pitch arrives as "you've probably already seen this".

**DO NOT:**
- Run a paid sponsorship ask — Scott has no announced commercial promo pipeline; asking looks off-pattern.
- Pitch Argumend as "Kialo but better" — the LW post explicitly names Kialo as the tool rationalists tried and rejected. Frame as *augmented prose synthesis*, not *graph-first UI*.
