# Solar Geoengineering / SAI / Marine Cloud Brightening — Research Dump

**Date:** 2026-05-19
**Scope:** Stratospheric Aerosol Injection (SAI), Marine Cloud Brightening (MCB), and adjacent Solar Radiation Modification (SRM) approaches. Argument map seed for Argumend.

## TL;DR

Solar geoengineering is a fast-polarizing field. As of 2025-2026, three forces are colliding: (1) **private capital** entering at scale (Stardust Solutions raised $60M in October 2025 for SAI field trials by 2026; Make Sunsets is selling "cooling credits" from a Winnebago); (2) **Global South governments** (African Group, Pacific, Mexico, Colombia) plus 600+ scholars pushing a **Non-Use Agreement**; and (3) **mainstream science** (IPCC AR6, NAS 2021, UNEP 2023, EU 2024) converging on a "research yes, deployment no" middle position that nobody on the flanks is happy with. Harvard's flagship SCoPEx experiment was killed in March 2024 after Indigenous-led opposition. Termination shock — long the central deployment-skeptic argument — has been quantitatively softened in recent literature, shifting the live cruxes to governance feasibility, monsoon disruption, and moral hazard.

## Positions (8)

1. **Deploy-now / emergency-cooling.** Climate change is already catastrophic; SAI is cheap (~$2-10B/yr per Harvard / Belfer estimates), Pinatubo proved it works, ethical to deploy unilaterally if multilateral process fails. Held loudly by Make Sunsets (Luke Iseman), some EA-aligned funders, and a few academics on the long-tail.
2. **Research-yes-deployment-no (NAS / IPCC mainstream).** Endorsed by NAS 2021 "Reflecting Sunlight" report (recommending $100-200M / 5yr US research program), IPCC AR6 WG1, UNEP 2023 "One Atmosphere," EU 2024 chief scientific advisors. SRM is not ready and may never be wise, but we need the science to decide.
3. **Moral-hazard / mitigation-deterrence prohibition.** Research itself is dangerous because it normalizes deployment and saps political will from emissions cuts. Position of the 600+ Non-Use Agreement scholars (Biermann et al. 2022), Heinrich Böll Stiftung, CIEL.
4. **Governance-first / multilateral-only.** Don't deploy without binding international institutions; deployment without consent is climate colonialism. Strong at UNEA-6 (African Group, Pacific Islands, Mexico, Colombia, Fiji, Pakistan, Vanuatu); reflected in the failed Swiss UNEA-6 resolution.
5. **Indigenous-veto / consent-based prohibition.** Saami Council, Indigenous Environmental Network, HOME! Alliance: SRM cannot be tested or deployed on lands or skies of Indigenous peoples without FPIC, full stop. Stopped SCoPEx in Kiruna 2021 and at Harvard 2024.
6. **Termination-shock-is-overstated.** Parker & Irvine 2018 + follow-ups argue most termination-shock modeling uses unrealistic "full-throttle then cliff" scenarios; a moderate, ramped, multi-actor program could be wound down safely. Reframes the debate away from worst-case rebound toward governance.
7. **Localized/MCB-only carve-out.** Marine Cloud Brightening for coral reefs (Great Barrier Reef RRAP, Southern Cross University) is *not* global SRM — it's regional climate adaptation. Try to separate it legally and politically. Held by Australian reef science establishment.
8. **Tech-acceleration / private-deployment.** Startups (Stardust Solutions, Make Sunsets, Reflective) plus VC funders treat SRM as a deployable product, not a research question. Public-interest framing is window dressing; the real position is "ship it, regulate later."

## Evidence (15)

1. **Mount Pinatubo, 1991** — ~15-20 Mt of SO₂ injected into the stratosphere; ~0.5°C global cooling for ~1-2 years; ~3 W/m² radiative forcing. Cited universally as proof-of-concept that stratospheric sulfate cools the planet. [USGS], [NASA Earth Observatory]. ([USGS Pinatubo](https://pubs.usgs.gov/pinatubo/self/), [NASA](https://earthobservatory.nasa.gov/features/Volcano))
2. **Harvard SCoPEx history & cancellation (2014-2024).** Proposed 2014 by Frank Keutsch & David Keith; planned 2021 Kiruna balloon flight cancelled after Saami Council opposition; entire project shut down March 18, 2024 after advisory committee report. ([MIT Tech Review](https://www.technologyreview.com/2024/03/18/1089879/harvard-halts-its-long-planned-atmospheric-geoengineering-experiment/), [Salata Institute](https://salatainstitute.harvard.edu/an-update-on-scopex/))
3. **Make Sunsets deployment.** 163 balloon releases as of May 2025; claims to have offset 141,101 metric tons of CO₂-warming-equivalent; EPA "demand for information" issued April 2025; operates from a Winnebago in California; banned from Mexico after unauthorized Baja California release Dec 2022. ([Inside Climate News](https://insideclimatenews.org/news/18042025/epa-targets-sulfur-dioxide-balloons-startup/), [IEEE Spectrum](https://spectrum.ieee.org/solar-geoengineering-2669008650))
4. **Stardust Solutions (Israeli-US startup).** Founded 2023; raised $60M Series A in October 2025 (largest-ever SRM raise); total $75M; plans outdoor SAI field experiments starting April 2026; proprietary undisclosed reflective particle. ([Heatmap](https://heatmap.news/climate-tech/stardust-geoengineering), [CIEL](https://www.ciel.org/news/us-israeli-start-up-announces-reckless-solar-geoengineering-experiments-from-april-2026/), [MIT Tech Review Dec 2025](https://www.technologyreview.com/2025/12/10/1129079/how-one-controversial-startup-hopes-to-cool-the-planet/))
5. **Reflective / ARC funding.** Funded by Renaissance Philanthropy's Advanced Research for Climate Emergencies program; backers include Cohler Charitable Fund, LAD Climate Fund, Open Philanthropy. SRM funding overall ~3× increase in 2025. ([Geoengineering Monitor](https://www.geoengineeringmonitor.org/geoengineering-the-stratosphere))
6. **Degrees Initiative (formerly SRMGI).** $1.8M+ in modelling grants to 150 researchers in 21 Global South countries since 2018; 50-paper milestone reached 2024; explicitly positions Global South scientists to model SRM impacts on their own regions. Critics (Geoengineering Monitor) call it "Global Southwashing." ([Degrees](https://degrees.ngo/), [Geoengineering Monitor critique](https://www.geoengineeringmonitor.org/the-degrees-initiative))
7. **IPCC AR6 WG1 (2021).** SRM assessed across Chs 4, 5, 6, 8. Finding: conceptually possible to meet multiple climate goals but "large uncertainties, knowledge gaps, substantial risks, institutional and social constraints." At best a *supplement* to mitigation. ([C2G AR6 brief](https://c2g2.net/c2glearn-ipcc/))
8. **NAS 2021 "Reflecting Sunlight" report.** Recommends $100-200M / 5yr US transdisciplinary research program with governance framework: code of conduct, public registry, permitting for outdoor experiments. Outdoor experiments only if "critical knowledge cannot be obtained by other means." ([NAS](https://www.nationalacademies.org/projects/DELS-BASCPR-18-04/publication/25762))
9. **UNEP 2023 "One Atmosphere" report.** Published Feb 28, 2023. Conclusion: "near and mid-term large-scale SRM deployment is not warranted and would be unwise at this time." Calls for global scientific assessment, multilateral governance, stratospheric governance framework, inclusivity. ([UNEP](https://www.unep.org/resources/report/Solar-Radiation-Modification-research-deployment))
10. **EU chief scientific advisors, Dec 2024.** Recommend EU-wide deployment moratorium plus responsible research plus global governance system. First formal EU-level guidance. Position EU as "proactively negotiating" against deployment globally. ([EC Research](https://research-and-innovation.ec.europa.eu/news/all-research-and-innovation-news/solar-radiation-modification-technologies-cannot-fully-address-climate-change-and-responsible-2024-12-09_en), [Climate Home News](https://www.climatechangenews.com/2024/12/09/eu-should-push-for-global-deal-not-to-deploy-solar-geoengineering-advisors-say/))
11. **Mexico SAI ban, Jan 2023.** Secretariat of Environment and Natural Resources announced moratorium Jan 13 and prohibition Jan 17, 2023, after Make Sunsets' unauthorized Baja California release Dec 2022. First national-level SRM ban. ([Climate Home News](https://www.climatechangenews.com/2023/01/18/mexico-plans-to-ban-solar-geoengineering-after-rogue-experiment/), [MIT Tech Review](https://www.technologyreview.com/2023/01/20/1067146/what-mexicos-planned-geoengineering-restrictions-mean-for-the-future-of-the-field/))
12. **Non-Use Agreement initiative.** Launched Jan 17, 2022 by Biermann et al. with 60+ initial signatories; now 600+ scholars from 67 countries plus ~1900 civil society orgs. AMCEN (African Ministerial Conference on the Environment) endorsed Aug 2023. ([SolarGeoeng.org](https://www.solargeoeng.org/non-use-agreement/), [WIREs Climate Change](https://wires.onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1002/wcc.754))
13. **UNEA-6, Feb 2024.** Switzerland withdrew SRM resolution after Global South coalition (African Group, Colombia, Fiji, Mexico, Pakistan, Vanuatu) blocked even a knowledge-repository proposal. US, Saudi Arabia, Japan attempted to weaken existing CBD moratoria; failed. ([TWN](https://www.twn.my/title2/climate/info.service/2024/cc240302.htm), [PLOS Climate](https://journals.plos.org/climate/article?id=10.1371/journal.pclm.0000413))
14. **Marine Cloud Brightening at the Great Barrier Reef.** Southern Cross University + Reef Restoration and Adaptation Program; Feb-Mar 2025 deployments at Broadhurst Reef and Fantome Island; electric drive high-pressure fogging system; ANU policy brief shows solar PV could power MCB with 59% emissions cut. Framed as reef adaptation, not geoengineering. ([SCU](https://www.scu.edu.au/research/reefs-and-oceans/marine-cloud-brightening/), [GBR Foundation](https://www.barrierreef.org/news/explainers/what-is-cloud-brightening))
15. **2024-2026 modeling literature.** Wang et al. 2026 (NASA GISS, ACP): SAI air-quality mortality impacts "modest, regionally heterogeneous, smaller than near-term air-policy gains"; ozone column loss ≤10 DU first 3 decades. Duffey et al. 2025 (Earth's Future): low-altitude high-latitude SAI feasible with existing aircraft. Afro-Asian monsoon studies (2025-2026): SRM reduces mean precip 5-7% in monsoon regions, with heavy-precip extremes falling and light-to-moderate wet days rising. ([Wang 2026 ACP](https://acp.copernicus.org/articles/26/1339/2026/), [Duffey 2025](https://agupubs.onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/full/10.1029/2024EF005567))

## Cruxes (6)

1. **Termination shock magnitude.** If SAI is started and abruptly stopped (war, financial collapse, AMOC scare), does the masked warming rebound catastrophically? Older GeoMIP G2/G3/G4 scenarios said yes; Parker & Irvine 2018 + later work say it depends on deployment magnitude and ramp profile. **Crux because:** if termination shock is real and large, you cannot ever safely start. If it's a modeling artifact of unrealistic scenarios, the deployment-skeptic case loses its strongest single argument.
2. **Regional precipitation / monsoon disruption.** Does SAI reduce summer monsoon rainfall in West Africa, the Sahel, South Asia, East Asia by enough to harm billions? Models show 5-7% mean reductions but disagree on sign and magnitude for specific basins. **Crux because:** if precipitation harms are concentrated in already-vulnerable regions, SRM becomes climate injustice at planetary scale; if effects are bounded and roughly comparable to unmitigated warming impacts, the cost-benefit flips.
3. **Governance feasibility.** Can ~195 sovereign states actually agree on, monitor, and enforce who deploys SRM, at what magnitude, where, and for how long — under what dispute-resolution regime? **Crux because:** every deployment scenario implicitly assumes one. UNEA-6 failure to agree even on a *knowledge repository* is the empirical update — and it cuts against the "research now, deploy under governance later" middle position.
4. **Moral hazard quantification.** Does SRM research / discussion measurably reduce political support for mitigation? Theoretical models (McLaren 2016) say yes; revealed-preference experiments (Merk & Wagner 2024, others) find little to no behavioral effect, sometimes a *galvanizing* effect. **Crux because:** if real, it justifies prohibiting even research; if absent, the strongest Non-Use Agreement argument weakens to "precautionary principle."
5. **Cost vs. mitigation.** SAI is genuinely cheap — ~$2-10B/yr operating, ~$18B per °C of cooling per UNEP — versus mitigation in the trillions. **Crux because:** the cheapness is what makes unilateral deployment possible (a single mid-sized state, a billionaire, a coalition of small island states could fund it) — that's also what makes it ungovernable. Cost cuts both ways depending on whether you trust collective action.
6. **Is MCB a separate question from SAI?** Marine Cloud Brightening at coral-reef scale (km², seasonal, near-surface) versus stratospheric, global, persistent SAI — are these the same regulatory category? **Crux because:** if you can legally and morally bracket reef-scale MCB as adaptation, you preserve a politically viable niche while keeping stratospheric SAI restricted. If they're treated as one category, MCB inherits SAI's political poison.

## Fallacies (5)

1. **Slippery slope ("research inevitably becomes deployment").** Used by Non-Use Agreement camp to argue against funding even small-scale outdoor experiments. Sometimes correct empirically (Stardust shows fast trajectory) but logically a slippery-slope when stated as inevitability rather than risk.
2. **Perfect-solution fallacy.** Used by deploy-now camp: "Mitigation alone won't stop warming, therefore SRM." Discards mitigation because it's imperfect, then ignores that SRM is also imperfect; doesn't follow that an imperfect supplement should be chosen *over* an imperfect base.
3. **Mitigation-vs-adaptation false dichotomy.** "We must choose between cutting emissions and modifying solar radiation." IPCC AR6 explicitly says SRM is *supplemental*; framing it as a substitute is rhetorical, not analytical. Both deploy-now and prohibition camps deploy this in different directions.
4. **Appeal to nature / appeal to hubris.** "Humans shouldn't play God / manipulate the atmosphere." Saami Council's framing has a coherent epistemic version (Indigenous knowledge of consequence-cascades) but is sometimes collapsed into a bare appeal-to-nature in popular discourse. Symmetric fallacy on the deploy side: "Pinatubo proved nature already does it," ignoring that nature also "does" pandemics.
5. **Hasty generalization from Pinatubo.** Pinatubo was a single short-lived pulse with measured cooling and known side-effects. Sustained, intentional, decadal-scale SAI is a different regime in chemistry (ozone), dynamics (jet streams), and politics (consent). Both sides cite Pinatubo selectively.

## Argumend topic notes

- **High graph density.** Many positions, asymmetric epistemic confidence, fast-moving evidence. Ideal for the Rich/Evidence/Meta node split.
- **Strong "side" structure** that does *not* map to left/right politics — the deploy-now and prohibition camps both cross-cut climate-left coalitions. Good demo of why bare 2-sided framings fail.
- **Live cruxes are quantitative** (termination shock, precip %, deployment cost, ozone DU), which lets the map show "this position depends on this number; here is the source." Better than purely values-based topics.
- **Indigenous/Global South positions** (Saami, AMCEN, Pacific Islands) are usually flattened in mainstream US coverage; surfacing them as first-class positions is a differentiator.
