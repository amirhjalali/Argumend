# Rationalist/EA Distribution Playbook — Case Studies & 30-Day Plays for Argumend

_Date: 2026-05-19_

## TL;DR

- The dominant cold-start pattern is **a single, well-crafted "Introducing X" post on LessWrong or the EA Forum, anchored to one tangible artifact** (a working tool, a survey, an issue-zero) — not a manifesto. Manifold, Fatebook, Rational Animations, Asterisk, and FRI all followed this template.
- The second pattern is **earn-then-launch**: build standing inside an existing venue (Computerphile, SSC comments, Twitter Spaces, ACX Grants) before announcing your own thing. Robert Miles, Liron Shapira, Scott Alexander, and AI Explained all leveraged borrowed audiences first.
- **Money plus social proof from Scott Alexander or Open Phil is a near-deterministic accelerant.** Manifold ($20k ACX Grant), Rational Animations (EAIF + Open Phil), Asterisk (Effective Ventures), and FRI (Open Phil) all had a sponsor named before users showed up. Argumend doesn't need the money — it needs the named endorsement and the LW launch post.

## Case studies

### Manifold Markets — Feb 2022
Founded Dec 2021 by Austin Chen + James & Stephen Grugett. Won an [ACX Grant](https://www.astralcodexten.com/p/acx-grants-results) ($20k seed) before product was finished, then launched via a [LessWrong post](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/qcYu6fMb6qg4zcftn/create-a-prediction-market-in-two-minutes-on-manifold) titled around the concrete action ("Create a prediction market in two minutes"). Found community. Led with the artifact, not the philosophy. ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Manifold_\(prediction_market\)))

### Fatebook (Sage) — 2023
[Launched on LessWrong](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/yS3d46m23wRKDQobt/introducing-fatebook-the-fastest-way-to-make-and-track) as "the fastest way to make and track predictions." Followed up with [Slack](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/3ZiemGGKASziKcxi5/fatebook-for-slack-track-your-forecasts-right-where-your) and [Chrome](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/vNhCpkSkuyARugJfn/fatebook-for-chrome-make-and-embed-forecasts-anywhere-on-the) extensions, each a fresh LW post — every distribution surface = a new launch. Pure "made" community: Sage built it for the LW/EA-org workflow.

### Asterisk Magazine — Nov 2022
[Announced on EA Forum](https://forum.effectivealtruism.org/posts/Mts84Mv5cFHRYBBA8/introducing-asterisk) by Clara Collier, funded by Effective Ventures. Issue 01 led with bylines from Scott Alexander, Kelsey Piper, Aella, Sarah Constantin — the rationalist canon, recruited as authors so they'd self-promote. ([RationalWiki](https://rationalwiki.org/wiki/Asterisk_Magazine))

### AI Impacts — 2014/15, with a viral spike 2022 + 2023
Katja Grace built slowly via the [2016 / 2022 / 2023 expert surveys](https://aiimpacts.org/wp-content/uploads/2023/04/Thousands_of_AI_authors_on_the_future_of_AI.pdf). The play: one shareable, citable empirical artifact per year, posted on LW + tweeted into the AI safety graph. Found community. Becomes the source citation everyone else uses.

### 80,000 Hours podcast — Jun 2017
Rob Wiblin shipped from inside an already-trusted org. Cold-start was already-warm: 80k had ~5 years of career-guide audience. Playbook = ride EA's existing distribution, lead with high-status guests (MacAskill, Tetlock, Ord, Christiano). Found community, then expanded. ([80000hours.org](https://80000hours.org/podcast/))

### Roots of Progress — 2017
Jason Crawford started as a [solo hobby blog](https://blog.rootsofprogress.org/about) on history of technology. No EA/LW prior. Made his own community ("Progress Studies") that later overlapped with rationalists. Built credibility via book reviews + long-form pieces, then Tyler Cowen / Patrick Collison amplification did the rest. ([Noahpinion interview](https://www.noahpinion.blog/p/interview-jason-crawford-nonprofit))

### Astral Codex Ten — Jan 21 2021
Not a cold start — Scott migrated 10 years of SSC audience to Substack with one [farewell post on SSC](https://slatestarcodex.com/2021/01/21/introducing-astral-codex-ten/) pointing at the new URL. Lesson for us: an audience pivot to a new product is a one-post operation if you already have an audience. We don't, yet.

### Robert Miles — 2014–17
The textbook **borrowed-audience play**. Did AI safety segments on [Computerphile](https://theinsideview.ai/rob) (1M+ subs) from 2015. Spun off his own channel only after recurring viewers were already asking "where's the longer version?" Channel never had a cold start because the audience was pre-warmed by Computerphile guest spots.

### Rational Animations — May 2021
"Writer" + co-founder launched with [a LW post](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/yekoZcQQfuZYqk3Bj/introducing-rational-animations) explicitly asking the community **which LW posts to adapt next**. Got the EAIF grant in April 2021 before going public. Genius move: framed the channel as a **service to LW canon**, not a competing brand — so LW writers became distribution allies, not rivals.

### AI Explained ("aiexplained-official") — early 2023
Channel exploded with the [SmartGPT / GPT-4](https://www.youtube.com/@aiexplained-official) early-access analysis videos. Playbook: be first to a high-attention model release with a genuinely-investigative breakdown. Not a community insider — a news-hook creator who got adopted by the community because the analysis was tighter than anyone else's. Now 350k+ subs.

### Liron Shapira / Doom Debates — Jun 2024
[Self-described](https://lironshapira.substack.com/p/liron-frame-fellowship-talk) playbook: (1) prove credibility on Twitter Spaces vs. accelerationists (notably outperformed Yudkowsky in a Hotz debate), (2) take "doomer" slot on Jubilee + Dr. Phil to get mainstream face-time, (3) THEN launch the show because viewers were asking for it. Earn-then-launch.

### Forecasting Research Institute — Dec 2022
[EA Forum launch post](https://forum.effectivealtruism.org/posts/kEd5qWwg8pZjWAeFS/announcing-the-forecasting-research-institute-we-re-hiring), Tetlock as named principal, Open Phil-aligned funding, hiring announcement bundled in. The post _is_ the launch — paired with the [Existential Risk Persuasion Tournament](https://forum.effectivealtruism.org/posts/orhjaZ3AJMHzDzckZ/results-from-an-adversarial-collaboration-on-ai-risk-fri) as the artifact. Made community around an empirical research program.

### Patterns across all 12
1. **One concrete artifact at launch.** A tool you can use in 2 minutes, an issue 0, a survey result, a debate clip. Not "we're building X."
2. **LW or EA Forum is the launch surface.** Even Substack-natives (ACX) and YouTube-natives (Rational Animations, Doom Debates) cross-post a launch announcement there.
3. **Borrow before you build.** Computerphile→Miles, Twitter Spaces→Liron, SSC→ACX, Open Phil grant→FRI/Rational Animations. The community trusts vouching more than promotion.
4. **Recruit the canon as contributors.** Asterisk got Scott + Aella + Kelsey to write Issue 01. Rational Animations adapts existing LW posts. Argumend's equivalent: have ACX/LW writers do a guest-mapped argument.
5. **News-hook timing beats slow burn for media products.** AI Explained on GPT-4; AI Impacts on the survey moments.

## Plays for Argumend — next 30 days

Ranked by expected user/signal yield per unit effort. Effort: S (≤1 day), M (2–5 days), L (1–2 weeks).

1. **Ship one LW post titled "Introducing Argumend: argument maps for the AI 2027 / [current controversy] debate."** (Effort: M.) Lead with a single live argument map of the most-discussed AI/EA controversy of the week, not the platform. Include one screenshot, one link to interact, one "what we'd love feedback on." Mirror to EA Forum. Pattern: Manifold, Fatebook, FRI launch posts.

2. **Apply for an [ACX Grant](https://www.astralcodexten.com/p/apply-for-an-acx-grant-2024) (or the next round) explicitly to fund the "AI risk debate atlas."** (Effort: S.) Even without funding, the application itself is a forcing function and Scott reads them. A grant + namecheck is the highest-leverage social-proof event available. Pattern: Manifold's pre-product ACX grant.

3. **Cold-email 8 named LW/ACX/EA writers (Scott Alexander, Zvi, Kelsey Piper, Katja Grace, Liron Shapira, Robert Miles, Daniel Kokotajlo, Jason Crawford) offering a curated argument map of THEIR most-cited post.** (Effort: M.) Pre-build the maps; ship as a gift, not an ask. If even 1 shares it, that's the Asterisk/Rational-Animations play in miniature.

4. **Pick one weekly news hook and ship a sub-24-hour "argument map of $event" thread on X + LW.** (Effort: M, recurring.) AI Explained's playbook. Each map is the artifact; the thread is the distribution. Build a 4-week cadence so people learn to expect it. Hooks for May–Jun 2026: any Anthropic/OpenAI safety paper, any Tetlock/FRI report, any new Yudkowsky-vs-accelerationist exchange.

5. **Run a public "Doom Debates argument map" partnership with Liron Shapira.** (Effort: S to ask, M to deliver.) Offer to map his next episode live, in exchange for a 30-sec mention. He's actively trying to grow rigor in AI-risk discourse — Argumend is on-mission for him. Pattern: Miles→Computerphile, Liron→Twitter Spaces (borrowed audience).

6. **Post a "calibration check / steelman" feature to Fatebook + Manifold communities.** (Effort: M.) Build the smallest possible integration: paste a Manifold market URL → Argumend generates the crux map of YES vs NO arguments. Then post about it on LW. You're now part of the prediction-market tool graph, not competing with it. Pattern: Fatebook's per-surface re-launches.

7. **Publish one "annual artifact" — `Argumend Survey: What rationalists actually disagree about, 2026`.** (Effort: L, but high signal.) Steal AI Impacts' play. Survey 100–300 LW/ACX commenters on 20 controversies, publish the disagreement map. Becomes a citable thing other people reference. Even at n=50 it's defensible.

8. **Guest-post on Asterisk + a Substack like Astral Codex Ten Open Thread.** (Effort: S–M.) Pitch a single piece: "What the AI debate looks like as a map" with embedded interactive. Asterisk publishes EA-adjacent contributors regularly; ACX Open Threads accept self-promotion if it's substantive. Pattern: Asterisk recruiting the rationalist canon.

### Sequencing for the 30 days
- **Week 1:** Plays 1 + 4 (launch post + first weekly hook). Draft ACX Grant app (play 2).
- **Week 2:** Plays 3 + 5 (gift maps to named writers + Liron pitch).
- **Week 3:** Play 6 (Manifold/Fatebook integration). Continue play 4.
- **Week 4:** Play 8 (Asterisk pitch). Start play 7 (survey design). Submit ACX Grant.

## Open questions

- Does Scott Alexander still link tools in his Open Thread / Mantic Monday / Highlights round-ups? Worth confirming current cadence — that's the single highest-yield organic placement.
- Is an EA Forum post still on equal footing with LW for a tool launch in 2026, or has the AI-pause/policy split changed the audience composition? Check first-week karma on recent comparable launches.
- Liron Shapira openness to mapping partnership — would he see Argumend as ally or as competing-for-attention? Probably ally given his stated mission, but unverified.
- AI Explained channel exact launch date and earliest viral video — couldn't confirm; would sharpen the "news-hook first video" recommendation.
- Whether the FRI / XPT data could be re-published as Argumend maps under permissive licensing — could be a single highest-leverage content drop.
