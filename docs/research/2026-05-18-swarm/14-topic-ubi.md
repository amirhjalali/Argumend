# Topic: Universal Basic Income (UBI)

**Date:** 2026-05-18
**Scope:** Positions, evidence, cruxes, fallacies, and 2024–2026 developments for an Argumend topic graph.

## TL;DR

UBI is no longer a thought experiment — by mid-2025 at least 122 US guaranteed-income pilots had distributed ~$481M to >40,000 recipients,[^aei] and the flagship 3-year OpenResearch RCT (Altman-funded, $1,000/mo) reported in July 2024.[^cbs] Yet the empirical picture is *less* triumphant than 2019-era advocacy promised: recipients work slightly *less*, mental-health gains fade, and large pilots show small negative employment effects (-3.2pp in the four largest US trials).[^aei] The debate has bifurcated into a "cash works, scale it" camp (GiveDirectly, Stockton, Yang) and an "evidence is mid, opportunity cost is huge" camp (Cass, Zvi, AEI). The underlying *cruxes* — labor-supply elasticity, inflation pass-through, fiscal financing, and the moral status of work — remain unresolved.

## Positions (8)

1. **Full UBI replacing the welfare state (libertarian / Murray).** Charles Murray's *In Our Hands* (2006/2016): give every adult ≥21 a flat $10,000/yr cash grant and abolish Medicaid, SNAP, Social Security, housing assistance, etc. Cash > paternalism; eliminates bureaucratic deadweight; restores civil society.[^murray]
2. **UBI on top of existing welfare (progressive / Standing).** Guy Standing, BIEN, most US guaranteed-income advocates: UBI is an *additional* income floor, not a replacement. Means-tested programs stay; UBI adds universal unconditionality.[^bien]
3. **Negative Income Tax / EITC expansion (Friedman / Niskanen).** Phase-out tied to earnings; cheaper than universal payment; preserves work incentive at margin. Friedman: "A basic income is just a NIT plus a positive income tax with no exemption."[^niskanen]
4. **AI-funded UBI (Altman / Yang).** Tax AI productivity / VAT / "compute dividend" to fund $1,000/mo as automation displaces labor. Yang's Freedom Dividend: ~$2.8T/yr funded by 10% VAT.[^yang] Altman frames OpenResearch as a precursor to a post-AGI transition.[^altman-blog]
5. **Sovereign-wealth dividend (Alaska model).** Don't redistribute income; redistribute the rents from common resources (oil, spectrum, data, AI). Alaska PFD pays ~$1,000–2,000/yr from oil fund; no measurable employment drop.[^jones]
6. **Job guarantee instead of UBI (Cass / MMT-left).** Oren Cass (American Compass): work is constitutive of dignity and social order; cash-without-work corrodes both. Replace UBI with a federal job guarantee at minimum wage.[^cass] MMT-left (Tcherneva, Wray) reaches similar policy from different premises.
7. **Work-requirement / conditional cash (centrist).** Hybrid: cash floor conditional on work, training, or care responsibilities. Closer to EITC + CTC expansion than UBI. Dominant US revealed-preference position (Romney CTC, 2021 expanded CTC).
8. **"UBI is a bad idea, period" (fiscal/inflation hawks).** Even if behaviorally neutral, a $3T/yr unfunded transfer crowds out investment, raises rates, or causes inflation; means-tested transfers dominate on every margin. Argued by Hassett (AEI), Furman, and Zvi Mowshowitz's *Don't Worry About the Vase* read of OpenResearch.[^zvi]
9. **Post-work / abundance UBI (left-accelerationist).** Srnicek/Williams, Bastani: UBI is the bridge to a fully-automated economy where wage labor withers. Not a poverty program; a liberation tool. Less mainstream but ideologically distinct from (2).

## Evidence (13)

| # | Study / data | Year | Headline finding | Citation |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| E1 | **OpenResearch (Altman) 3-yr RCT** — $1,000/mo to 1,000 Texans/Illinoisans vs. $50 control (n=2,000) | 2024 | Recipients worked **~1.3–1.4 hrs/wk less**; labor force participation **−2pp**; mental-health/food-security gains in year 1 **faded by years 2–3**. Spending went to basics + helping family. | [Vivalt et al. NBER][vivalt][^cbs][^zvi] |
| E2 | **GiveDirectly Kenya long-term UBI** — 23,000 people, 195 villages, 12-yr commitment | 2018–ongoing; first results 2023 | Long-term UBI + lump-sum both effective; **no idleness**; recipients more entrepreneurial, higher earnings. Strong stabilizer during COVID. | [Banerjee/Faye/Krueger/Niehaus][gd-paper][^mit] |
| E3 | **Finland 2017–2018 trial** — 2,000 unemployed, €560/mo unconditional | Final report 2020 | **Tiny positive employment effect**; large gains in life satisfaction, trust, mental health. Confounded by mid-trial "activation model" reform. | [Kela / Ministry of Social Affairs][finland] |
| E4 | **Stockton SEED** — 125 residents, $500/mo for 24 mo | 2019–2021 | **Full-time employment ↑ from 28% → 40%** (control 32% → 37%); depression/anxiety fell. Small n. | [West et al., Penn SP2][seed] |
| E5 | **Alaska Permanent Fund Dividend** — ~$1–2k/person/yr since 1982 | Synthetic-control 2018, replicated | **No effect on aggregate employment**; part-time work +1.8pp (17%). General-equilibrium demand effect offsets individual labor-supply pullback. | [Jones & Marinescu, AEJ:EP 2022][jones] |
| E6 | **Ontario Basic Income Pilot** — 4,000 people, up to C$17k/yr | 2017–2019 (cancelled early) | Small decline in labor-force participation; large QoL gains. Cancelled by Ford government before RCT data collected → class-action ongoing. | [Wikipedia / Hamilton report][ontario] |
| E7 | **MGNREGA (India rural job guarantee)** — 100 days work guarantee, ~1.3% of GDP | 2005–present | Reduces poverty, raises wages, improves school attendance; functions as wage floor. Costs ~₹860B/yr — relevant as the **scaled alternative** to UBI advocated by Cass-style critics. | [3ie working paper 27][mgnrega] |
| E8 | **Banerjee et al. meta-analysis** — 7 cash-transfer RCTs (developing countries) | 2017 | **No systematic evidence cash transfers reduce employment.** Robust across Kenya, Nicaragua, Honduras, Philippines, Indonesia, Morocco, Mexico. | [Banerjee/Hanna/Kreindler/Olken, World Bank Research Observer][banerjee] |
| E9 | **Penn Wharton Budget Model** — hypothetical $1.5T/yr US UBI, 3 financing scenarios | 2018, updated | Debt-financed UBI: GDP **−6.1% by 2027**, capital stock falls. Tax-financed: smaller hit. Bond-market response is the largest macro lever, not behavioral. | [PWBM][pwbm] |
| E10 | **AEI synthesis of 30 randomized US GBI pilots** | 2025 | Avg employment effect **+0.8pp**; but in the **4 largest pilots** (≥500 participants, 55% of recipients): **−3.2pp**. Smaller pilots biased upward. | [Strain & Greszler, AEI][aei] |
| E11 | **Alaska PFD labor elasticity (Feinberg & Kuehn)** | 2018 | Microeconomic labor-supply elasticities: men −0.10 to −0.15; single women −0.09 to −0.14; married women −0.11 to −0.18. | [Urban Institute / Semantic Scholar][feinberg] |
| E12 | **Jain Family Institute "Guaranteed Income Pilots Dashboard"** — 122 pilots, $481M, 40k+ recipients | 2017–2025 | Largest meta-tracking effort; finds heterogeneity dominated by program design (amount, duration, targeting), not "UBI works/doesn't." | [JFI / Stanford Basic Income Lab][jfi] |
| E13 | **Welsh care-leavers trial** — £1,600/mo to young people leaving foster care | 2022–2026 | Ongoing; preliminary survey data shows improved housing stability, mental health, no work-effort drop in 18–24 cohort. Concludes Nov 2026. | [Welsh Government][wales] |

## Cruxes (5)

**C1. Labor-supply elasticity at scale.**
*If* a national UBI causes <5% drop in hours worked, fiscal math is tractable. *If* >10%, dynamic scoring breaks the program. Evidence is **mixed and context-dependent**: developing-country RCTs say ~0; OpenResearch says −2pp LFP and −1.3 hrs/wk; Alaska PFD says ~0 aggregate but negative individual elasticity offset by demand spillover. **Disagreement on which environment generalizes to a US national rollout.**[^vivalt][^jones][^banerjee]

**C2. Replacement vs. addition.**
A Murray-style replacement UBI is *redistributively regressive at the bottom* (recipients of Medicaid/SNAP/SSDI lose more than $10k). A Standing-style additive UBI is *fiscally enormous* ($3T+/yr). Almost every disagreement between left and libertarian UBI camps collapses to this crux.[^murray][^bien]

**C3. Inflation pass-through.**
Does $1,000/mo to every adult bid up rents, groceries, and labor-intensive services? PWBM general-equilibrium model says modestly; ground-up monetarist critics say a lot; MMT advocates say only if you exceed real productive capacity. **No clean empirical test exists** because no rich country has run a UBI universally — pilots by design hold prices fixed.[^pwbm]

**C4. Funding mechanism.**
VAT (Yang) vs. wealth tax (Sanders-adjacent) vs. carbon dividend (Hansen) vs. sovereign-wealth / AI-compute tax (Altman) vs. deficit. Each has different incidence, growth effect, and political feasibility. The position someone holds on UBI usually traces back to which tax they already wanted.[^yang][^altman-blog]

**C5. Moral status of work.**
Is paid work intrinsically dignifying (Cass) or contingently necessary (Standing, Srnicek)? Empirics can't settle this — but it determines whether "people worked slightly less" reads as cost or benefit. OpenResearch results were treated as a win by advocates ("more time with kids, training, caregiving") and a loss by critics ("dependency, deconditioning").[^cass][^scott]

## Common fallacies (5)

**F1. Cherry-picking pilot size.** Citing Stockton (n=125, full-time employment up) without citing OpenResearch (n=1,000, employment down). Or vice versa. AEI's 2025 meta makes this explicit: pilot size correlates with sign of employment effect.[^aei]

**F2. Pilot-to-national extrapolation.** A $500/mo transfer to 125 people in a $200B economy has no general-equilibrium effect. A $1,000/mo transfer to 250M adults does. Treating Stockton as a forecast for national UBI is a **scale fallacy**.

**F3. Free-money fallacy (begging the question on financing).** Advocates cite GDP-growth benefits without specifying tax base; critics cite cost without netting out abolished programs. Both sides routinely compare a financed program to an unfinanced baseline.

**F4. Naturalistic appeal to work.** "Humans need to work to be fulfilled" treated as empirical when it's normative. Cass-style arguments often slide between these registers.[^cass]

**F5. Survivorship/silence on the Ontario pilot.** Advocates cite cancellation as "proof Conservatives feared positive results"; critics cite cancellation as "proof the program was already failing." Neither claim is supported — **no outcome data was collected.**[^ontario]

## 2024–2026 developments

- **July 2024 — OpenResearch results published.** Vivalt, Miller, Rhodes, Bartik et al. The most-anticipated UBI RCT in history landed with mixed reviews. Popular press: positive. Academic consensus per Zvi Mowshowitz and AEI: between mixed and disappointing.[^cbs][^zvi]
- **2025 — AEI 122-pilot synthesis.** First comprehensive employment-effects meta of US GBI pilots. Reframed the field: pilot size predicts sign of effect.[^aei]
- **2025 — Guaranteed Income Pilot Program Act (HR 5830).** Rep. Watson Coleman re-introduced; 3-year national pilot, payment tied to fair-market rent of 2BR in zip code. Did not pass.[^hr5830]
- **2025 — Connecticut HB 6841.** State-level 1-yr UBI pilot in 5 municipalities (raised bill, status pending).[^ct]
- **2026 (ongoing) — Welsh care-leavers trial concludes November.** First publicly-funded UBI-style program in the UK.[^wales]
- **GiveDirectly Kenya** continues toward its 12-year terminal data (~2029). Mid-trial COVID data published 2023 in *The Review of Economic Studies*.[^gd-paper]
- **Altman post-OpenResearch posture (2025–26).** Has softened from "AGI requires UBI" to advocating "universal basic compute" / equity-in-AI-systems — a notable shift from cash to in-kind.[^altman-blog]
- **Andrew Yang's Forward Party** continued to push UBI in 2024 cycle but with diminished policy salience; Freedom Dividend largely absent from 2024 presidential discourse.
