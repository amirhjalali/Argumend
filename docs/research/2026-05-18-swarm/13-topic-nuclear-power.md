# Nuclear Power Debate — Research Dump

**Date:** 2026-05-19
**Topic ID:** nuclear-power
**Sources:** primary citations inline; web search via Lazard, EDF, NRC, Constellation, Talen SEC filings, World Nuclear News, Our World in Data, UNSCEAR, IAEA.

---

## Framing

The 2024–2026 nuclear debate is no longer purely climate-vs-safety. It now turns on four live questions: (1) can Western new-build economics be fixed, (2) will SMRs ship at a competitive LCOE before 2032, (3) do hyperscaler PPAs change the financing model, and (4) is fusion close enough to delay fission decisions. AI/data-center load growth has shifted the political coalition: tech capital and labor unions now flank traditional pro-nuclear conservatives, while degrowth greens and ratepayer-advocacy groups flank traditional anti-nuclear environmentalists.

---

## Positions (8)

### P1. Pro-build, all-of-the-above (SMR + AP1000 + restarts)
Build everything that gets through licensing — large LWRs (AP1000, APR-1400), Gen-III+, SMRs (NuScale, BWRX-300, Holtec SMR-300), and Gen-IV (Natrium, Kairos). View: cost overruns are fixable through fleet learning (FOAK→NOAK), and AI load growth makes any zero-carbon firm capacity worth ~$110–140/MWh. Champions: DOE, hyperscalers, Nuclear Energy Institute.

### P2. Pro-build, large reactors only (no SMR optimism)
Skeptical of SMRs after NuScale-UAMPS cancellation [1]. Argues only proven Gen-III+ designs (AP1000, APR-1400, EPR2) and Korean/UAE-style serial construction work. SMR economics fail because you lose the scaling law (cost ∝ MW^0.6) without offsetting modularization gains. Advocates: Energy economists like Jessica Lovering's pre-SMR work, IEA scenarios.

### P3. Pro-build, restart and uprate existing fleet only
The cheapest decarbonization MWh is a re-licensed reactor. Restart Palisades [9], Three Mile Island Unit 1 [6], Duane Arnold; extend 60→80-year licenses; uprate to 105–110% thermal. Don't bet on new builds until a NOAK ships under budget. Champions: Constellation, Holtec, much of the IEA's "Nuclear's Comeback" thesis.

### P4. Anti-build, economics-based
Not opposed to nuclear in principle — opposed to spending $35B+ per project when Vogtle 3&4 cost $36.8B vs $14B original [2], Hinkley C is now £35B (~£48.7B inflation-adjusted) and slipped to 2030 [3], and Lazard 2025 LCOE for new nuclear is $141–220/MWh vs $38–78/MWh utility solar+storage firm [10]. Position: every nuclear dollar displaces ~3× MWh of solar+wind+storage+transmission. Champions: Amory Lovins, Mark Z. Jacobson (electricity-only argument), ratepayer advocates.

### P5. Anti-build, safety/waste/proliferation
Classical anti-nuclear: Fukushima evacuation cost ~$200B+ social damage even with low radiological mortality; no operating permanent waste repository outside Onkalo [12]; SMR proliferation surface area expands with HALEU enrichment. Champions: Union of Concerned Scientists, Greenpeace, IPPNW.

### P6. Pro-build only under public ownership
Nuclear's risk profile (long horizons, large capex, externalized waste liability) only closes under sovereign balance sheets. Evidence: France's 2023 EDF renationalization for €9.7B to fund six EPR2 reactors [11]; TVA's reactor-restart and SMR program; Korea's KEPCO/KHNP state-backed serial build. Private utility builds (Vogtle, V.C. Summer cancelled, NuScale) chronically blow up. Champions: democratic-socialist climate writers, French government, parts of UK Labour.

### P7. Fusion-first / fusion-only optimists
Don't lock 60–80-year fission capital when Commonwealth Fusion Systems' SPARC targets Q>1 in 2027 and a 400 MW ARC plant in Chesterfield, VA in the early 2030s [14], plus Helion, TAE, Tokamak Energy. Google signed a 200 MW PPA with CFS in July 2025 [14]. View: spend the next decade on grid + storage + fusion R&D, skip the fission bridge. Champions: fusion startups, parts of the tech-VC climate wing.

### P8. Hyperscaler-financed nuclear (new political economy)
The interesting 2024–2026 development: Microsoft signed a 20-year PPA with Constellation for the restarted Three Mile Island Unit 1 (835 MW) [6]; Amazon bought the Cumulus campus next to Talen's Susquehanna for $650M [7]; Google contracted Kairos Power for 500 MW of SMRs by 2035 [8]. View: hyperscaler PPAs solve the "no creditworthy offtaker" problem that killed prior new-build attempts. Counter from P4: FERC rejected the Talen-Amazon expanded ISA in Nov 2024 [7], suggesting co-location may not scale.

## Evidence (15)

### E1. Lazard LCOE+ June 2025 — nuclear new-build $141–220/MWh
Lazard's 2025 LCOE+ report shows unsubsidized LCOE for new nuclear at $141–220/MWh, down 1% YoY. Utility-scale solar standalone is ~$29–$92/MWh; solar + 4-hr storage ~$60–210/MWh; CCGT ~$48–109/MWh. [10] (lazard.com/media/eijnqja3/lazards-lcoeplus-june-2025.pdf)

### E2. Vogtle 3 & 4 final cost: $36.8B vs $14B original
Two Westinghouse AP1000 units at Plant Vogtle in Georgia cost $36.8B vs $14B original estimate (~163% overrun), 7 years late. Unit 3 commercial Jul 31 2023, Unit 4 Apr 29 2024. Georgia residential rates rose 23.7% post-COD. [2] (en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vogtle_Electric_Generating_Plant; georgiarecorder.com)

### E3. NuScale-UAMPS Carbon Free Power Project cancelled Nov 2023
Target power price went from $55/MWh (2021) to $89/MWh (Jan 2023); construction estimate rose $5.3B → $9.3B (75% jump) for a 462 MW plant. Killed for insufficient subscribers. Only NRC-certified US SMR design at the time. [1] (utilitydive.com; catf.us)

### E4. TerraPower Natrium construction starts in Wyoming
NRC issued construction permit March 2025; Bechtel began construction at Kemmerer Unit 1. 345 MW sodium-cooled fast reactor with molten-salt storage to 500 MW peak. Total private raise >$2.2B since 2022 (incl. $650M in June 2025). Target completion 2030. First Gen-IV commercial build in US. [4] (terrapower.com; power-eng.com; ans.org)

### E5. Westinghouse AP300 SMR — 2027 design certification target
300 MWe pressurized SMR based on AP1000. UK Generic Design Assessment in progress; agreement with Community Nuclear Power Ltd Feb 2024 for 4 units in North Teesside. NRC pre-app underway; design certification targeted 2027, first unit late decade. [5] (westinghousenuclear.com; world-nuclear-news.org)

### E6. Hinkley Point C: £35B (£48.7B inflation-adjusted), first power 2030
Feb 2026 EDF announcement: cost £35B (2015 prices) ≈ £48.7B today, vs £18B original (2016). First power moved from 2029 best-case to 2030 (could slip to 2031). EDF took €2.5B impairment in 2025 results. [3] (newcivilengineer.com; bloomberg.com)

### E7. French fleet recovery 2024–2025
Nuclear output 373 TWh in 2025 (vs SCC-crisis low of ~279 TWh in 2022). Winter 2024–25 availability 85%, >50 GW. 15 of 16 SCC-priority reactors repaired by end 2023. Cour des comptes: maintenance cost rose 28% over decade to €6.4B/yr; availability 74% vs 80% prior decade. [11] (sfeninenglish.org; world-nuclear-news.org)

### E8. Korean APR-1400 at Barakah: ~$5,700/kW, 48-month build
4 × 1,400 MW units in UAE for ~$32B total (≈$5,700/kW). Unit 4 entered commercial operation Sept 2024. Build time approximately on the original ~48-month-from-first-concrete plan — the cleanest large-reactor build of the 2010s–2020s. Benchmark for P2 (large-reactor pro) and P6 (state-backed) positions. [iaea, en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Barakah_nuclear_power_plant]

### E9. Deaths per TWh — nuclear 0.03, coal 24.6, solar 0.02
Our World in Data / Markandya & Wilkinson + Sovacool et al.: coal ~24.6 deaths/TWh (mostly air pollution); oil ~18.4; gas ~2.8; nuclear ~0.03; wind ~0.04; solar ~0.02. Nuclear is statistically as safe as solar/wind, ~700× safer than coal per unit energy. [13] (ourworldindata.org/safest-sources-of-energy)

### E10. UNSCEAR Chernobyl/Fukushima mortality
Chernobyl: 28 acute-radiation deaths in first 3 months among 134 ARS cases; ~19,000 thyroid cancers among childhood-exposure cohort (substantial fraction attributable to radiation). Fukushima: no excess cancer detectable; thyroid screening increase is screening artifact, not radiation-driven. Thyroid doses ~100× lower than Chernobyl. [unscear.org; who.int]

### E11. Onkalo, Finland — first deep geological repository
Encapsulation trial completed early 2025 (5 spent-fuel canisters placed at 430m depth). Full startup mid-2020s, operational by 2026. World's first permanent spent-fuel disposal site. IAEA DG Grossi: "game changer." Yucca Mountain in US remains unfunded/stalled. [12] (nucnet.org; iaea.org; en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Onkalo_spent_nuclear_fuel_repository)

### E12. Microsoft–Constellation Three Mile Island restart PPA
Sept 20 2024: 20-year PPA, 835 MW, largest nuclear PPA in operator history. Renamed Crane Clean Energy Center. ~$1.6B capex for restart; $1B DOE loan secured Nov 2025. Target service: 2028 pending NRC review. [6] (sec.gov 8-K; constellationenergy.com; cnbc.com)

### E13. Amazon–Talen Susquehanna co-location: FERC rejected expansion
March 2024: AWS acquired Cumulus data campus next to Talen Susquehanna for $650M. Existing ISA permits 300 MW co-located load. Nov 1 2024: FERC rejected amended ISA that would have raised co-location to 480 MW. Status as of 2025: AWS proceeds at 300 MW; expansion remains pending. [7] (sec.gov 8-K; utilitydive.com)

### E14. Google–Kairos Power 500 MW SMR agreement
Oct 14 2024: Google PPA for ~500 MW from Kairos Power's KP-FHR (fluoride salt-cooled, high-temp) reactors. First unit (Hermes 2) target online 2030; full 500 MW fleet by 2035. Hermes demo reactor under construction at Oak Ridge, TN. First hyperscaler advanced-reactor PPA. [8] (blog.google; world-nuclear-news.org)

### E15. ITER delayed, Commonwealth Fusion accelerating
ITER (Cadarache, France): July 2024 baseline revision pushed first plasma to 2034 (D-D), full D-T to 2039, +$5.2B over prior overruns (now ~$10B+ above original). Counter-trend: Commonwealth Fusion's SPARC targets Q>1 in 2027; 400 MW ARC plant planned in Chesterfield, VA early 2030s; Google PPA for 200 MW signed July 2025. [14, 15] (physicsworld.com; cfs.energy)

### E16. Palisades restart — first US reactor un-shutdown
Holtec returned Palisades (Covert Twp, MI; 800 MW PWR) to "operational status" Aug 2025 — first time a fully shut-down US reactor has been brought back. Restart slipped from 2025 to early 2026. $1.52B DOE loan guarantee + $400M for two co-located SMR-300 units. [9] (nucnet.org; michiganadvance.com)

## Cruxes (6)

### C1. Will SMR NOAK pricing fall below $80/MWh?
**Pro-build (P1, P3)**: yes, by 2032 — factory-built, modular, fleet learning. **Anti-build (P4)**: NuScale already failed at $89/MWh as a target. SMR economics need ≥10-unit factory orders that don't exist outside KEPCO. **Resolution data**: TerraPower Kemmerer 1, BWRX-300 (Darlington), Holtec SMR-300 (Palisades) actual capex per kW circa 2030.

### C2. Does hyperscaler PPA financing actually unblock new build?
**Pro (P1, P8)**: Microsoft/TMI, Google/Kairos, Amazon/Talen show creditworthy offtake exists. **Con (P4)**: FERC Nov 2024 Talen rejection [7] suggests grid regulators won't allow load-shifting away from the public grid. Plus: existing-fleet PPAs (TMI, Susquehanna) don't add new MW; they reroute them. **Resolution**: FERC's pending decision on co-location frameworks (expected 2026) + first new-build SMR with hyperscaler offtake reaching FID.

### C3. Is Vogtle/Hinkley a Western institutional pathology or a real cost floor?
**P2, P6**: Korean APR-1400 at $5,700/kW [E8] proves serial state-led construction works; Vogtle/Hinkley reflect lost Western nuclear skills, fragmented supply chains, and equity-financed risk. **P4**: even Korean costs are now rising; no Western country has the institutional muscle to replicate KEPCO. **Resolution**: EPR2 fleet (France, six units to 2035) and AP1000 if any restart in US.

### C4. Does waste management still block political consent?
**Pro (P1)**: Onkalo [E11] proves geological disposal works engineering-wise; deaths-per-TWh [E9] proves operational safety. **Con (P5)**: 47 US states without a repository; political risk of HALEU stockpiles for SMRs; intergenerational ethics. **Resolution**: number of new repository siting decisions (Sweden's Forsmark, France's Cigéo, Canada's Ignace) reaching construction by 2030.

### C5. How big is the AI load gap, and does only nuclear fill it?
**Pro (P1, P8)**: data center demand growing ~15%/yr; intermittent + storage can't deliver 24/7 PUE-stable power; nuclear is the only firm zero-C source. **Con (P4)**: geothermal (Fervo), long-duration storage, and demand response can fill the gap at lower $/MWh. **Resolution**: 2027–2029 actual hyperscaler procurement mix.

### C6. Is fusion close enough to defer fission?
**P7**: SPARC Q>1 in 2027, ARC 400 MW early 2030s [E15]. **P1, P2**: ITER's slip to 2039 shows fusion timelines are perpetually 10 years out; even CFS optimistic case = 1 commercial plant in 2032 vs need for ~100 GW new firm capacity. **Resolution**: SPARC net-energy demonstration result; ARC permitting milestones.

## Fallacies (5)

### F1. LCOE-as-only-metric (anti-nuclear side)
Comparing nuclear $141–220/MWh [E1] to solar $29–92/MWh ignores firmness, capacity value, transmission, and land-use. Lazard itself includes "firming cost" adjustments, but these are routinely stripped in advocacy. The Breakthrough Institute has called this out specifically against Lazard's methodology [lazard 2025; thebreakthrough.org].

### F2. NuScale-implies-all-SMRs-fail (anti-nuclear side)
NuScale's cancellation [E3] was a single 462 MW project at a specific cost/subscriber profile. Treating it as a verdict on all SMR designs (BWRX-300, Natrium, Kairos KP-FHR, Holtec SMR-300, AP300) conflates one product failure with a category failure. P2 makes this argument structurally; P4 sometimes makes it rhetorically.

### F3. Chernobyl-implies-Western-LWR-risk (anti-nuclear side)
Conflating an RBMK-1000 graphite-moderated Soviet reactor without containment with modern PWR/BWR designs. UNSCEAR's 28 acute-radiation deaths [E10] are real; transferring that risk profile to AP1000 or APR-1400 ignores 4 decades of design evolution.

### F4. Korea/France-proves-it-works-anywhere (pro-build side)
Citing Barakah at $5,700/kW [E8] as proof Western new-build can hit similar economics ignores KEPCO's vertical integration, sovereign-backed financing, single-design serial construction, and labor-cost differences. Same fallacy in reverse direction as F3 — institutional transfer is harder than design transfer.

### F5. Hyperscaler-PPA-solves-financing (pro-build side)
Microsoft/TMI [E12] and Google/Kairos [E14] are real, but Three Mile Island is a restart of existing capacity, not new MW; the Talen-AWS expansion was blocked by FERC [E13]. Extrapolating from 2–3 announcements to "the financing problem is solved" is premature. Position P8 borders on this fallacy without the FERC caveat.

### F6. Fusion-just-around-corner (P7 risk)
ITER's slip from 2025 first plasma to 2034 [E15] is the latest of ~6 decades of 30-year-away projections. CFS's faster timeline is credible but unproven; the gap between SPARC Q>1 (lab demo) and ARC 400 MW commercial is the gap that has historically destroyed fusion programs.
