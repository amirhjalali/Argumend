# Distribution: Academic & Education Adoption Playbook

**Date:** 2026-04-28
**Analyst:** Research agent 09/10 — distribution sweep for Argumend
**Topic:** Debate clubs, philosophy/critical-thinking departments, edtech distribution channels. Where Kialo Edu found its million users, and which doors Argumend can realistically open without LMS integrations or a sales team.

## 1. The Debate Ecosystem

The competitive forensics world is structurally tiny relative to its cultural footprint, and almost all of the addressable population sits inside three or four named organizations whose calendars are public.

**National Speech & Debate Association (NSDA)** is the gravitational center of US scholastic debate. Founded 1925, headquartered in West Des Moines. It serves **~140,000 students and coaches per year, with roughly 1,500 schools sending teams to NSDA Nationals 2024 (6,700 student qualifiers)**, and tracks 2M+ alumni ([NSDA About](https://www.speechanddebate.org/), [Aralia guide](https://www.aralia.com/helpful-information/guide-to-the-nsda-nationals/)). The NSDA owns the topic-selection wording committees for **Lincoln-Douglas (one-on-one philosophical), Public Forum (two-on-two current-events, the largest format by participation), Policy (cross-examination, the format with the deepest research demands), Big Questions, World Schools, and Congress**. The wording committee meetings are where prep-material companies (Champion Briefs, DebateUS, Premier Debate) pitch to be the official briefs vendor — that is the analogue access point for an AI tool.

**National Debate Coaches Association (NDCA)** publishes the Novice Packet — the canonical introductory case set for the Policy resolution, currently 2025-26 Arctic ([NDCA Novice](https://www.debatecoaches.org/resources/novice)). NDCA is small and coach-run; the entire community lives on a Facebook group of a few thousand coaches. **Whoever the NDCA recommends gets adopted across hundreds of high schools in a single email.**

**National Association for Urban Debate Leagues (NAUDL)** runs **~10,154 students across 22 cities through 20 affiliated leagues; 76% low-income, 86% students of color** ([NAUDL Who We Are](https://urbandebate.org/who-we-are/), [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/National_Association_for_Urban_Debate_Leagues)). Funded by foundations (Open Society, Spencer, Carnegie). The 2025 Urban Debate National Championship was at Emory; NAUDL's 2025 "Sports Debate" pilot suggests they are actively looking for novel topic-formats. **NAUDL is the single most leverageable scholastic-debate gatekeeper for a free AI tool because (a) the league is centrally administered, (b) coach turnover is high so they need pre-built materials, and (c) their philanthropic funders explicitly want innovation.**

**National Parliamentary Debate Association (NPDA)** is the dominant intercollegiate parliamentary format: one tournament per year, late March, hosted at Mercer in 2026 ([NPDA](https://npdadebate.com/)). APDA runs the East Coast Ivy circuit. Both are small (~50-80 schools) but produce disproportionate alumni.

**International Baccalaureate Theory of Knowledge (TOK)** is the single largest critical-thinking-as-a-class population globally. **180,000+ IB Diploma students take TOK each May exam cycle** ([Amerigo Education](https://www.amerigoeducation.com/news/ib-exams)) — TOK is mandatory for all DP students. TOK exists in 5,000+ IB World Schools across 160 countries. Kialo Edu's single most valuable acquisition channel was guest posts by IB TOK teachers (Hafsa Khan, etc.) — and the IB Global Conference (Washington DC 2024) is where they met those teachers. **AP Seminar and AP Research (the College Board's "AP Capstone" program) are the closest US analogue** — argument analysis, evidence-based research, formal defense — though enrollment is an order of magnitude smaller than IB.

**Model UN** is much larger than usually appreciated: **400,000+ students worldwide** ([Wikipedia: MUN](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Model_United_Nations)). Major conferences: NHSMUN (NYC, 3,500+ delegates), THIMUN Hague, Harvard MUN, Harvard MUN India (1,700+), MUNUC Chicago. MUN is research-heavy and position-paper-driven — directly compatible with argument-mapping.

**National High School Ethics Bowl (NHSEB)**, headquartered at UNC-Chapel Hill's **Parr Center for Ethics**, runs ~24-team national championships annually, with regional competitions feeding through a new Divisional Playoff system; registration $175 early / $250 late ([NHSEB FAQ](https://nhseb.org/faq), [Parr Center](https://parrcenter.unc.edu/nchseb/)). Ethics Bowl is structurally different from debate — collaborative analysis rather than adversarial — and **its case library is publicly published, which makes it a perfect free corpus for Argumend to pre-analyze and seed the platform with**.

The collective addressable population is roughly **200K US scholastic debaters + 180K IB TOK + 400K MUN + ~100K Ethics Bowl/AP Seminar/college parliamentary = ~900K students globally**, all concentrated through a handful of named organizations and 6-8 annual conferences.

## 2. Philosophy & Critical Thinking Department Adoption

Curriculum adoption at the postsecondary level looks nothing like edtech adoption at K-12.

**The textbook publishers' chokehold is real but eroding.** Pearson, Cengage, McGraw-Hill, Macmillan/Bedford and Oxford UP own the introductory-logic / critical-thinking textbook market — Patrick Hurley's *A Concise Introduction to Logic* (Cengage), Lewis Vaughn's *The Power of Critical Thinking* (Oxford), Kahane/Cavender (Cengage). New editions every 3-4 years, $150+ price tags, instructor inspection-copy programs, and bundled homework systems (MyLogicLab, Connect, Achieve). Adoption decisions are made by individual instructors at small schools and by department committees at R1s, typically in May for the following fall.

**OER (Open Educational Resources) is the structural threat to that model and Argumend's natural ally.** The California Community Colleges OER Initiative (ASCCC-OERI) has explicit philosophy and critical-thinking working groups ([ASCCC OER Philosophy](https://asccc-oeri.org/open-educational-resources-and-philosophy/)). Mercy College documented post-course critical-thinking gains using OER materials — students using OER scored 5.73 on critical-reading post-tests vs. 4.99 in control sections ([Springer OER review](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s11423-016-9434-9)). OER Commons, Pressbooks (eCampusOntario), MERLOT, and Open Textbook Library are the distribution surfaces. **An Argumend "argument mapping companion" listed on OER Commons with a CC-BY license costs us nothing, requires no salesperson, and ranks for "free critical thinking textbook" — which is what an adjunct teaching three sections at $3,200 each Googles in August.**

**Critical thinking course adoption committees** at large universities (the GE / Gen-Ed committee) are composed of 6-12 faculty across departments, meet 2-4 times per year, and almost never invite vendors. The way in is through a single sympathetic philosophy professor who pilots the tool in one section and presents results to the committee. **This is exactly how Tim van Gelder seeded Rationale at Melbourne, and how Maralee Harrell seeded argument diagramming at Carnegie Mellon.** Both have published quasi-experimental data.

**The argument-mapping literature is the playbook.** Van Gelder's meta-analysis claims **0.8-standard-deviation gains on critical-thinking measures from high-intensity argument-mapping courses, vs. 0.34 SD for typical critical-thinking instruction** (Abrami et al.) ([van Gelder 2015](https://thinkeranalytix.org/wp-content/uploads/2018/09/TvG-Using-argument-mapping-to-improve-critical-thinking-skills-2015.pdf)). Harrell's 269-student CMU quasi-experiment found significant effects of argument diagramming on argument-analysis performance ([Harrell, KiltHub](https://kilthub.cmu.edu/articles/journal_contribution/Argument_Diagramming_and_Critical_Thinking_in_Introductory_Philosophy/6490934)). The 2025 Nesbit systematic review in *Higher Education Quarterly* coded 124 studies ([Wiley](https://doi.org/10.1111/hequ.70063)). **This is rare in edtech: an actual evidence base, owned by friendly academics, published before AI argument extraction existed. Argumend's pitch writes itself — "we automate the manual mapping van Gelder/Harrell proved works."**

## 3. Edtech Distribution Channels

**Common Sense Education paused its review program in February 2026** after running 2013-2025 and reviewing 3,000+ tools, redirecting to AI risk assessments ([Tech & Learning](https://www.techlearning.com/technology/apps/common-sense-education-will-pause-edtech-reviews-beginning-february-2026-what-it-means-for-schools-and-where-to-look-next), [Common Sense FAQ](https://www.commonsense.org/education/reviews/FAQ)). The lists, Selection for Learning badges, and edtech reviews **are being removed from the site entirely in February 2026**. This is a structural change in the edtech landscape — the most-cited free review service is gone. The survivors that already reviewed Kialo and similar tools favorably are **EdTech Impact** ([edtechimpact.com](https://edtechimpact.com/)), **Digital Promise** (which runs ESSA Tier 3 product certifications), **TeachersFirst**, and **edWeb.net**.

**ISTE (International Society for Technology in Education)** — flagship K-12 edtech conference. **ISTELive 26 is June 28-July 1, Orlando, co-located with ASCD Annual** ([ISTE 2026](https://conference.iste.org/2026/)). 2025 booth pricing was **$5,460 per 10x10 booth before Aug 31, $6,495 after** ([ISTE Exhibit](https://conference.iste.org/2025/exhibitors/index.php)). With travel, materials, and staff: realistically **$15-25K all-in for a small booth**. Kialo Edu has been a regular exhibitor (ISTE 2025 booth 3229).

**BETT Show (London, late January)** is the international counterpart. **BETT Awards** are the most prestigious edtech recognition globally; Kialo Edu won **Free Digital Content of the Year 2025** ([Bett Awards](https://bettawards.com/categories/)). The 2026 winners and shortlist are public — submission window typically opens June, closes August/September.

**ESSA Evidence Tiers I-IV** are the gold standard for US public-school adoption. Tier I requires a randomized controlled trial with statistically significant effects; Tier IV (the entry tier) requires only a logic model and an active research plan ([IES WWC](https://ies.ed.gov/ncee/wwc/essa), [Digital Promise Tier 3](https://digitalpromise.org/product-certifications/evidence-based-edtech-essa-tier-3/)). **Kialo Edu holds ESSA Level IV.** Tier IV is achievable in ~3-6 months of writing and partnering with one university researcher.

**LMS integrations** are the boring core. Schools run on **Canvas (Instructure), Blackboard (Anthology), D2L Brightspace, Schoology (PowerSchool), and Moodle**. The relevant standard is **LTI 1.3 / LTI Advantage** with Names and Roles Provisioning + Assignment and Grade Services ([Canvas LTI docs](https://www.canvas.instructure.com/doc/api/file.tools_intro.html)). The Moodle plugin directory is open-submission ([Moodle Plugins: Kialo Discussion](https://moodle.org/plugins/mod_kialo)) — Kialo's plugin source is public on GitHub ([kialo/moodle-mod_kialo](https://github.com/kialo/moodle-mod_kialo)). **Moodle Certified Integration** status (which Kialo earned) is a paid program but provides a marketing flywheel.

**Long-tail content/blog channels** that produce teacher-discovery traffic: **TCEA TechNotes**, **TeachersFirst**, **Tech & Learning**, **edWeb.net** (1M+ educator subscribers), **Edutopia**, **ASCD's Educational Leadership**, **ISTE's EdTech magazine**, **Rachelle Dené Poth** (rdene915.com). All accept teacher guest posts — Kialo has placed at every one.

## 4. Kialo Edu Playbook Recap

Kialo Edu's path from 0 to 1M users (2019 → 2025) is documented in its own quarterly retrospectives. The mechanics are unglamorous and replicable:

- **2019-2021:** Pivoted kialo.com (general-public structured-debate site, launched 2017) into kialo-edu.com walled garden, free-forever, no ads, no data sale. Funding: undisclosed private foundation, no VC ([Kialo Edu Pricing](https://www.kialo-edu.com/pricing-why-kialo-is-free)).
- **2022-2023:** Teacher-blog seeding. Guest posts on TCEA, TeachersFirst, Tech & Learning, edWeb. APSA pedagogy paper by Charity Butcher ([APSA PDF](https://educate.apsanet.org/wp-content/uploads/Creating-Online-Debates-Using-Kialo-Edu.pdf)). Hit 500K users by end of 2023.
- **2024:** **Conference push** — MoodleMoot Global 2024 (Mérida, Gold Sponsor), EDUtech Asia 2024 (Singapore), IB Global Conference 2024 (Washington DC). **Integrations sprint** — LTI 1.3 for Canvas, Blackboard, D2L Brightspace shipped early 2024; Moodle Certified Integration; Small Group Mode shipped. **Awards** — HundrED Global Collection 2024; Austrian Ministry of Education's *Gütesiegel Lern-Apps* (Learning Apps Seal of Approval) ([Kialo 2024 Highlights](https://blog.kialo-edu.com/announcements/celebrating-kialo-edus-2024-highlights/)). 700K users by year end.
- **2025:** **The Austrian deal** — installed as a default Moodle integration across the entire Austrian school system, the single largest distribution win in Kialo's history, downstream of the Gütesiegel and the MoodleMoot relationship. **BETT Award 2025** (Best Free Digital Content). **AASL Best Digital Tools 2025**. **EU Digital Education Hub Accelerator 2024**. **ISTE 2025 booth 3229** ([Kialo 2025 so far](https://blog.kialo-edu.com/announcements/what-kialo-has-been-up-to-in-2025-so-far/)). 1M users.

**Three pattern observations:**

1. **The big win came from a small country.** Austria has ~1.1M students. By being the default Moodle plugin in one centrally-administered system, Kialo got hundreds of thousands of users in a single bureaucratic decision. The lesson: target smaller national education ministries that run a unified Moodle/Canvas instance (Estonia, Norway, Iceland, Slovenia, NZ).
2. **The flywheel is awards → integration → conference presence → award.** No single channel works alone. ESSA Level IV got the schools to listen, BETT got the press, MoodleMoot Gold Sponsorship got the Austrian Ministry call.
3. **Teacher-bloggers are unpaid sales.** Hafsa Khan (IB TOK teacher) writing a guest post on the Kialo blog about TOK use cases is worth more than a paid ad — it ranks for long-tail search.

## 5. Argumend's Realistic Foothold

Without LMS integration, teacher accounts, classroom management, or a sales team, Argumend cannot run the Kialo playbook end-to-end. It can run a wedge.

**The realistic wedge is "AI-pre-analyzed argument library, free, link-shareable, no login."** That is what Argumend already is. The use cases where this is sufficient:

- **AP English Language & Composition rhetorical-analysis prep.** The course's three Big Ideas are Rhetorical Situation, Claims and Evidence, Reasoning and Organization ([AP English Lang Course](https://apcentral.collegeboard.org/courses/ap-english-language-and-composition)). Students need exemplars of decomposed arguments. Argumend's pre-analyzed maps of canonical AP-style arguments (op-eds, speeches, essays) are a direct study aid. Population: ~550K AP Lang test-takers/year.
- **IB Theory of Knowledge.** TOK essays require multiple "areas of knowledge" perspectives on a knowledge question. Argumend can pre-publish maps for each year's six prescribed essay titles — students cite Argumend as a starting brainstorm, not as the final source. Population: ~180K TOK students/year.
- **Philosophy 101 / Intro to Logic / Critical Thinking 100-level courses.** Adjuncts assigning supplementary materials Google "free argument map example fallacies." Argumend's 109+ pre-analyzed topics with explicit fallacy nodes are exactly that. Population: ~500K US college students per year in some critical-thinking-required course.
- **Debate prep — Lincoln-Douglas and Public Forum.** Each NSDA topic cycle (monthly for PF, bi-monthly for LD) generates 100K+ student researchers. Argumend pre-analyzing the official resolution within 48 hours of release is a uniquely AI-enabled move Kialo cannot match. Population: ~75K active high-school debaters in those formats.
- **NHSEB case library mirroring.** Ethics Bowl publishes its case set; Argumend pre-analyzes each case as a position-evidence-crux map. Population: ~5K student competitors but disproportionately influential teachers.

**What Argumend cannot credibly do without product investment:** classroom management, grading, FERPA-compliant student rosters, student data isolation, anonymous student mode. These are weeks-to-months of work. **It can defer all of them by positioning as "study supplement, not classroom platform."** That positioning lets it bypass district IT review, vendor procurement, and the FERPA/COPPA gauntlet entirely — students access it like they access Wikipedia.

## 8. Watch-Outs

**Long sales cycles.** Even "free" K-12 tools require district IT / privacy / instructional review. Typical timeline: teacher discovers tool September → uses it personally October-December → asks IT to whitelist January → IT review March-May → district approval following August. **For Argumend that is 12 months from first teacher impression to district-approved use.** Kialo took 6 years to get to 1M. Patience is not optional.

**FERPA / COPPA exposure.** The instant Argumend asks a student under 13 for an email, COPPA applies (verifiable parental consent, separate kids' privacy notice). The instant a teacher uses it as part of a graded assignment with student-identifying data, FERPA-style protections apply. Schools now use ~1,449 distinct edtech tools per district on average and are aggressively cutting that list ([Secure Privacy](https://secureprivacy.ai/blog/school-data-governance-software-ferpa-coppa-k-12)). **The cleanest mitigation is to keep Argumend login-less and treat it as a study reference, not a classroom platform — until the company is ready to build proper student-data isolation.**

**"Free for teachers" still requires sales motion.** Kialo's Brooklyn/Berlin team of 60+ is mostly engineering and customer-facing. Their "free forever" pricing page is sales copy, not an absence of selling — they pay for booth space at MoodleMoot, BETT, ISTE; they court ministry officials; they write guest posts. A solo-founder pre-revenue startup cannot replicate this without choosing 1-2 channels and going deep.

**The Common Sense Education review pause is a one-time arbitrage opportunity that ends.** Schools that previously checked Common Sense before approving a tool now have nowhere to look — TeachersFirst, EdTech Impact, and Digital Promise will absorb that traffic over 6-18 months. Argumend should submit to all three *now*, before reviewer queues lengthen.

**Sterile classroom positioning conflicts with the rationalist/X audience.** Per the Kialo competitive-intel research (file 01), Argumend's most natural early-traction audience is the LessWrong/SSC/Manifold rationalist crowd who actively reject sterile classroom debate aesthetics. The education playbook in this document points the other direction — neutral, evidence-based, ESSA-certified. **These audiences cannot be served by the same landing page.** Plan separate funnels (e.g., argumend.org/edu vs argumend.org/debates) or risk alienating both.

**The IB is centralized but slow.** IB curriculum revisions happen on 5-7 year cycles; the next TOK guide revision is 2027. Getting a tool referenced in the official guide is functionally a decade-long project. Targeting IB TOK *teachers* (rather than the IB organization) is the only realistic path.

**The argument-mapping research base is older than current AI.** Van Gelder's effect-size claims are based on Rationale (manual-mapping) studies from 2000-2010s. Argumend's AI extraction is a different intervention; the existing literature does not directly validate it. **Leaning too hard on van Gelder's numbers risks researcher pushback that "your tool is doing the cognitive work students are supposed to be doing."** This is a real critique and Argumend should have a defensible answer (e.g., AI-extracted map as a *starting scaffold* students then critique and modify, not a final product).
