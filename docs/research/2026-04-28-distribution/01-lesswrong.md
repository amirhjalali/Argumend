# Distribution Research: LessWrong

**Date:** 2026-04-28
**Analyst:** Research swarm 01/10 — distribution channel scan for first 1000 users

## 1. Snapshot

LessWrong is a long-running rationalist forum at lesswrong.com, founded 2009 around Eliezer Yudkowsky's "Sequences," rebooted as **LessWrong 2.0** in 2017 by Oliver Habryka, Ben Pace, and the team that later spun out as **Lightcone Infrastructure** (the rebrand was announced October 2021 by Habryka on X) ([Oliver Habryka tweet](https://x.com/ohabryka/status/1443751665966874624)). Lightcone now operates LessWrong, runs the **Lighthaven** event venue in Berkeley, and runs the **Inkhaven** writing residency.

**Operations and people, April 2026:**
- **Run by:** Oliver Habryka (CEO of Lightcone), with Ben Pace and the LW dev team. Lightcone is hiring generalists/programmers starting at $150k/year ([Habryka, X 2021](https://x.com/ohabryka/status/1443751665966874624)).
- **Funding:** Per Habryka's 2026 fundraiser post "Toss a bitcoin to your Lightcone," Lightcone needs ~$2M to maintain operations in 2026, aiming to raise $3M. 2025 actual spend was $3.1M ($3.6M Lighthaven expenses including a $1M one-time mortgage interest payment, plus ~$1.7M for LessWrong and other projects, offset by $3.2M Lighthaven program revenue). Funded primarily by non-institutional donors; Coefficient Giving, SFF, FLI consider them cost-effective but won't fully fund. ([Toss a bitcoin to your Lightcone](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/eKGdCNdKjvTBG9i6y/toss-a-bitcoin-to-your-lightcone-lw-lighthaven-s-2026)).
- **Ad revenue:** ~$40k/year (negligible).

**Traffic and activity, late 2025 / early 2026:**
- **2025 unique users:** ~4.2M (Habryka: "almost twice as many users visit this year as previous years, ~65% more sessions and views") ([Lightcone fundraiser 2026](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/eKGdCNdKjvTBG9i6y)).
- **2025 page views:** ~22M.
- **November 2025 alone:** 748 posts published.
- **AI 2027 single scenario** generated 745,423 engagement hours in 2025.
- **Similarweb Nov 2025:** global rank ~51,279, US rank ~20,272, Philosophy category rank #11. Direct traffic 54.74%, then Organic Search; social referrers led by X, then Reddit, then YouTube ([Similarweb lesswrong.com](https://www.similarweb.com/website/lesswrong.com/)).
- **Semrush Apr 2025:** 625.3K visits/month, average session 10:28 ([Semrush](https://www.semrush.com/website/lesswrong.com/overview/)).

**Structure (not "subforums" in the Reddit sense — LW uses tags + a Frontpage/Personal split):**
- **Frontpage** = curator-promoted posts surfaced to all users by default.
- **Personal** = author-published, default-only-to-followers, anything-goes.
- **Curated** = mod-starred subset (about 3 per week) on top of Frontpage ([Site Guide: Personal vs Frontpage](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/5conQhfa4rgb4SaWx/site-guide-personal-blogposts-vs-frontpage-posts)).
- **Core tags:** Rationality, AI, World Optimization, Practical, Community, Site Meta ([The Core Tags](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/KnHPrP2K8SewomJY6/the-core-tags)).
- **Open Threads:** quarterly now ("Open Thread Spring 2025," "Open Thread Fall 2024") — used to be weekly, frequency stepped down ([Spring 2025 OT](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/bg3LBMSuEhi52kNBQ/open-thread-spring-2025)).

**Recent dynamics (last 12 months):**
- **Inkhaven 1** (Nov 2025): 41 residents, all completed 30 posts in 30 days. Inkhaven posts averaged 50 karma vs 27.8 karma for non-Inkhaven in Nov 2025 — Inkhaven authors wrote 212 of 748 (28%) posts that month ([Inkhaven Residency](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/CA6XfmzYoGFWNhH8e/the-inkhaven-residency)).
- **Inkhaven 2** announced for April 2026 ([Announcing Inkhaven 2](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/nwWfsPiaFSiEtHbkJ/announcing-inkhaven-2-april-2026)).
- **LLM policy controversy:** "The New LessWrong LLM Policy is Worse Than You Think" ([LW post](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/JjzdRJjwLQKWkXakC/the-new-lesswrong-llm-policy-is-worse-than-you-think)) — mods adjusted moderation policy around AI-written content; this matters for Argumend because **AI-generated argument maps may run into the same skepticism**.

## 2. Audience Overlap with Argumend

LessWrong is, plausibly, the highest-density audience on the internet for "nerds who care about argument structure." The community has been workshopping debate-mapping primitives for over 15 years.

**Specific evidence the audience cares:**

| Concept | LW post | Karma | Date | Why it matters |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Double Crux | ["Double Crux — A Strategy for Resolving Disagreement"](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/exa5kmvopeRyfJgCy/double-crux-a-strategy-for-resolving-disagreement) by Duncan Sabien | 196 | 2017-01-02 | Argumend's "crux" node taxonomy is literally named after this CFAR/LW concept |
| Argument mapping | ["Argument Maps Improve Critical Thinking"](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/NJ8k2RTwy3ELmwYZT/argument-maps-improve-critical-thinking) by Johnicholas | 26 | 2009-08-30 | Cites Twardy's CCTST research; foundational LW post on the topic |
| Debate tools | ["Debate tools: an experience report"](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/dJJYgmaYYFmHoQM4L/debate-tools-an-experience-report) by Morendil | 51 | 2010-02-05 | LW user tries Argunet and bCisive; concludes "categorization scheme matters more than visualization" — directly relevant to Argumend's typed-node thesis |
| Kialo critique | ["Kialo: An online discussion platform that attempts to support..."](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/g3odvaj8opqCF9egv/kialo-an-online-discussion-platform-that-attempts-to-support) by mirefek | 10 | 2017-11-05 | LW community pre-rejected Kialo's pro/con format — opening Argumend can fill |

**The crux concept is more than a coincidence:** Duncan Sabien's 196-karma 2017 post canonized "double crux" as the rationalist disagreement-resolution technique. CFAR built workshops around it. The basic structure — find a B such that B↔A and ¬B↔¬A for both parties — is exactly what Argumend's cruxtacean agent extracts. **A LW post titled "We built an LLM agent that finds double cruxes — here are 3 it found in real essays" is essentially a perfect fit, in a way that almost no other product has.**

**Estimated overlap:** Of LW's ~4.2M annual unique users in 2025, the active core (people who post/comment regularly) is much smaller — likely 5–15K monthly active commenters based on observed comment volume. Of those, the subset who care specifically about argument-mapping tooling is small but high-signal: think hundreds to low thousands. **The right unit of measurement isn't "% of LW users" but "% of LW frontpage posts that could be plausibly cited by Argumend's product."** That number is meaningfully high — anything tagged Rationality, Epistemics, AI Safety, or Forecasting is in the cone.

## 3. What Gets Traction on LessWrong

LessWrong karma rewards: technical depth, calibrated epistemic markers ("epistemic status: speculative"), object-level engagement with prior LW posts, and concreteness. It punishes: marketing voice, vague "community building" content, hot takes on US politics, low-effort AI-generated text.

**Recent (last 12 months) high-karma posts in adjacent areas:**

| Post | Karma | Author | Topic | Why it worked |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| ["A deep critique of AI 2027's bad timeline models"](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/PAYfmG2aRbdb74mEp/a-deep-critique-of-ai-2027-s-bad-timeline-models) | 273 | titotal | AI forecasting critique | Substantive technical critique of a high-status post; Daniel Kokotajlo updated his estimates publicly in response |
| ["Recent AI model progress feels mostly like bullshit"](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/4mvphwx5pdsZLMmpY/recent-ai-model-progress-feels-mostly-like-bullshit) | high | — | Empirical AI model evaluation | Concrete claims with eval data |
| ["Shallow review of technical AI safety, 2025"](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/Wti4Wr7Cf5ma3FGWa) | high | — | AI safety landscape | Comprehensive, citable |
| ["Introducing Squiggle AI"](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/7worWgggeHL3Eb7wq/introducing-squiggle-ai) | 92 | ozziegooen | Probabilistic estimation tool launch | Real working tool, tied to existing LW vocabulary (Squiggle/Guesstimate) |
| ["Announcing Squiggle: Early Access"](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/mv6nuN7nhnNpj9MLr/announcing-squiggle-early-access) | 51 | ozziegooen | Early-access tool launch | Honest about limits, responsive to feedback |
| ["I built a semantic search engine for LessWrong"](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/vDcJHD95XCg7ywANM/i-built-a-semantic-search-engine-for-lesswrong) | 13 | neo | Tool for LW itself | Underwhelmed — only 2 comments, no examples in post |
| ["I made a tool to bring LessWrong everywhere around the web"](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/tqfZgWG5yebKfRqbh/i-made-a-tool-to-bring-lesswrong-everywhere-around-the-web) | — | — | Chrome extension | Recent (Apr 2026), low engagement |

**Pattern:** the *form* that lands is "I am an insider, here is technical work I did, here are the limits." Not "Hi, I built X, please try it." The Squiggle launches (Ozzie Gooen) are the canonical example of how to introduce a serious tool to LW: he was already a known QURI/EA-Forum poster, named the tool in LW vocabulary ("Guesstimate as a programming language"), launched as "Early Access," acknowledged missing features in the comments rather than defending. **51 karma is a respectable LW launch outcome** for a niche dev tool.

## 4. Past Attempts: Products Launched on LW

**Kialo** — [posted 2017-11-05 by mirefek](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/g3odvaj8opqCF9egv/kialo-an-online-discussion-platform-that-attempts-to-support), 10 karma, 21 comments. Got picked apart in the comments: "tree structure loses context," "binary pro/con doesn't capture clarification or rope-pulling-sideways," "popularity vote ≠ accuracy," "doesn't screen for reasoning ability." A small post that established LW's anti-Kialo stance for a decade. **Lesson: Argumend should not look like Kialo.**

**Squiggle / Squiggle Hub / Squiggle AI** (Ozzie Gooen, QURI) — three separate launch posts (Aug 2022 Early Access 51 karma, Aug 2023 Squiggle Hub, Jan 2025 Squiggle AI 92 karma). The most successful product-launch sequence on LW in the epistemics-tools space. **Lesson: launch repeatedly with each substantive update; don't expect one big bang.** ([Squiggle Early Access](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/mv6nuN7nhnNpj9MLr/announcing-squiggle-early-access), [Squiggle AI](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/7worWgggeHL3Eb7wq/introducing-squiggle-ai)).

**Manifold Markets** — has a presence (the [Manifold Markets tag/post](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/ptEtB4wbLixRuy8MG/manifold-markets-1)) and integration: there's a bot that auto-creates Manifold markets on whether 100+ karma LW posts will make the annual review top 50 ([example market](https://manifold.markets/JeremyPerret/will-there-be-a-100-karma-lesswrong)). Manifold's traction came from being authored by people already embedded in the rationalist scene (James Grugett, Stephen Grugett, Austin Chen — Manifold is YC-backed) rather than from a single launch post. **Lesson: relationships > launches.**

**Metaculus** — embedded into LW via the [Embedded Interactive Predictions](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/JLrnbThMyCYDBa6Gu/embedded-interactive-predictions-on-lesswrong-2) feature. Metaculus integrated *into* the platform rather than just posting at it. **Lesson: technical integration with LW (oEmbed, embed widget) is a force multiplier.**

**Roam Research / Andy Matuschak / evergreen notes** — never had a "launch on LW" moment per se, but the Andy Matuschak / Conor White-Sullivan circle is rationalist-adjacent and Roam was discussed organically. No direct evidence of LW being a meaningful Roam acquisition channel.

**Arbital** (the most relevant cautionary tale) — built by Alexei Andreev, Eliezer Yudkowsky, and team 2014–2017 explicitly as a "Wikipedia for arguments," shut down 2017, [postmortem](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/kAgJJa3HLSZxsuSrf/arbital-postmortem) by alexei has **233 karma, 110 comments** on 2018-01-30. Lessons (almost all relevant to Argumend):
1. **Content creation is the bottleneck, not visualization.** People consume explanations; almost nobody writes them. Stack Exchange-style fast social feedback is what actually drives contribution.
2. **They prioritized engineering over user acquisition and demand validation.**
3. **Designing primarily for one user (Eliezer) created an atypical-use-case tool.**
4. **Feature creep beat traction every time.**
5. **Leadership ambiguity slowed decisions.**

Most of Arbital's IP eventually got [imported into LessWrong](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/fwSnz5oNnq8HxQjTL/arbital-has-been-imported-to-lesswrong) — as "Lenses" (alternative tabs on the same subject). **Argumend's biggest risk is repeating Arbital, not Kialo.**

## 5. Cultural Rules / How to Not Get Downvoted

The explicit-and-implicit norm stack:

- **Frontpage criteria** ([Frontpage Posting and Commenting Guidelines](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/tKTcrnKn2YSdxkxKG/frontpage-posting-and-commenting-guidelines)): "usefulness, novelty, and fun"; the "Victorian Sufi Buddha ideal" of accuracy, kindness, relevance; clarity about beliefs, reasons, and cruxes; descriptive over prescriptive language; **timeless over ephemeral**.
- **Frontpage avoids:** community-meta, hot-button politics, crowded topics already saturated elsewhere ([Avoid Unnecessarily Political Examples](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/bNAEBRWiKHG6mtQX8/avoid-unnecessarily-political-examples)).
- **Personal posts** are the opt-out for self-promotion-adjacent content. **Anything that feels like marketing should be Personal, not Frontpage.** Authors can mark this when posting.
- **New User Guide norms** ([New User's Guide](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/LbbrnRvc9QwjJeics/new-user-s-guide-to-lesswrong)): probabilistic thinking, "we applaud you for saying 'oops'," reference prior LW posts, use rationalist vocabulary correctly, address existing arguments. New users with negative karma get auto-rereviewed; sock puppets and vote manipulation are hard-banned.
- **Curated** (the gold tier): about 3 posts per week starred by mods as exceptionally insightful or instructive.
- **Open Threads** (quarterly now, e.g. [Spring 2025](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/bg3LBMSuEhi52kNBQ/open-thread-spring-2025)): the official channel for "minor details, off-topic, meta, or anything that isn't a full post." Often where new users introduce themselves. **A short product mention here is acceptable; a top-level post pitching your product is not.**
- **Crossposts from Substack/Medium/personal blog**: explicitly tolerated but warned against. From the LW FAQ thread: "many of these neither strike the team as particularly interesting nor demonstrate interest in LW's culture/norms or audience." If the post reads like a Substack repost with no LW-specific framing, it goes Personal at best, gets deleted at worst.
- **Marketing language is poison.** From the [LessWrong FAQ](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/2rWKkWuPrgTMpLRbp/lesswrong-faq): Eliezer in the Sequences "very rarely mentions his organization, instead focusing almost entirely on learning rationality skills" — and even *he* spelled out his ulterior motives explicitly because it's "crucial those motives not poison the integrity of the rationality project."
- **AI-generated content under more scrutiny since 2025–2026.** The recent [LLM Policy](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/JjzdRJjwLQKWkXakC/the-new-lesswrong-llm-policy-is-worse-than-you-think) controversy means visibly LLM-written posts get strong skepticism. **Argumend posting "look at this map our LLM extracted" needs to be very honest about what's machine-generated and why it's still epistemically valid.**
- **Habryka personally curates.** He and Ben Pace make Frontpage and Curated calls; their taste is the actual filter. Reading [habryka's user page](https://www.lesswrong.com/users/habryka4) and his recent comments is the cheapest way to learn what passes.

## 6. Concrete Actions Argumend Could Take

Ranked by effort × likely impact. Specific, not generic.

**A1 — [LOW EFFORT, MEDIUM IMPACT] Post a deep technical writeup of the cruxtacean agent in the Personal section, marked as such.**
Title suggestion: "We built an LLM agent that finds double cruxes — here are 3 it identified in recent ACX posts." Body: explain the prompt structure, show three real cruxes with the source paragraphs, name what the model got wrong, link to the live tool as the *third* link, not the headline. Submit on a **Tuesday or Wednesday** (highest LW traffic days), not weekend. Mark Personal, not Frontpage — let mods promote if they want. Cost: ~1 day of writing time. Likely outcome: 30–80 karma, 5–15 comments, 10–50 referral visits, 1–2 high-quality contacts.

**A2 — [LOW EFFORT, HIGH ASYMMETRIC PAYOFF] Post in the Open Thread Spring 2026 (or Summer 2026 when it goes up).**
A short comment: "Built an AI argument-mapping tool inspired by double crux + IBE. Free, no signup. Looking for harsh feedback from people who've thought about debate tooling. https://argumend.org/topics/[best-topic]" — link to one specific high-quality topic, not the homepage. Reply in the same comment thread to whoever engages, not in DMs. Cost: 30 minutes. Likely outcome: 5–20 karma comment, 3–10 commenters, of whom 1–2 will be substantive critics whose feedback is worth more than the user count.

**A3 — [MEDIUM EFFORT, MEDIUM IMPACT] Cross-pollinate with a known LW author.**
Identify 3–5 LW authors who have written about epistemics tooling (Ozzie Gooen, Raemon, johnswentworth, Duncan Sabien, titotal). Email them: "We built X; would you be willing to break it for us before we post?" Offer to credit them. **Ozzie Gooen specifically** is the highest-value target because (a) he's launched 3 epistemics tools on LW, (b) Squiggle/Guesstimate is conceptually adjacent, (c) he's responsive. Cost: a few hours of outreach. Likely outcome: even one engaged co-validator triples the launch post's credibility.

**A4 — [MEDIUM EFFORT, HIGH IMPACT] Apply to Inkhaven 2 (April 2026) or Halfhaven (online alternative).**
Inkhaven 1 had 41 residents at Lighthaven; Halfhaven was the online Discord version. Inkhaven authors averaged 50 karma per post vs 27.8 for non-Inkhaven in Nov 2025. **Becoming an Inkhaven resident is the single highest-leverage credibility move available on LW.** It also gives 30 days of forced posting, plus access to Scott Alexander, Gwern, Andy Matuschak, Adam Mastroianni, Aella, et al. as in-person mentors ([Inkhaven post](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/CA6XfmzYoGFWNhH8e/the-inkhaven-residency)). If 2026's window is closed, target 2027. Cost: 1 month + travel. Highest expected payoff of any item on this list.

**A5 — [MEDIUM EFFORT, MEDIUM IMPACT] Build a Metaculus-style oEmbed/embed widget.**
Argumend's argument-map node should be embeddable inside an LW post like Manifold Markets' embedded predictions. This requires the LW dev team to whitelist the embed (oEmbed standard). Email Habryka or Ben Pace. Cost: ~1 week dev. Payoff: every future post that references an Argumend topic gets a live, interactive map inline. ([Embedded Interactive Predictions feature](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/JLrnbThMyCYDBa6Gu/embedded-interactive-predictions-on-lesswrong-2) is the pattern to copy.)

**A6 — [HIGH EFFORT, HIGH IMPACT] Pre-extract one canonical LW debate, ship the map, post it.**
Pick a famously contested topic like AI doom probabilities or AI 2027 critiques. Run Argumend over the 5–10 highest-karma essays from both sides. Publish the resulting argument map *as a free reference artifact* — a "Wikipedia of the AI 2027 debate" — and post a Frontpage-eligible "I mapped the AI 2027 debate; here's what surprised me" writeup. Tie directly to the ongoing 273-karma titotal critique thread. Cost: 1–2 weeks of careful curation and writing. Payoff: this is the type of post that can hit Curated.

**A7 — [LOW EFFORT, MEDIUM IMPACT] Respond, don't pitch, in comment threads.**
On every future LW post about double crux, IBE, fallacies, AI debate, epistemics tooling — leave a substantive comment that says something useful first, *then* mentions Argumend if relevant. Track the recent activity around posts like "Contra double crux," "The Basic Double Crux pattern," and Squiggle AI's comment threads. Cost: ongoing, ~30 min per week. Compounds.

## 7. Watch-Outs

What gets a product torn apart, downvoted, or quietly Personal-ed:

- **Marketing-voice prose.** "Argumend revolutionizes how you think about debate" — instant downvotes. Use plain, calibrated, slightly self-deprecating language: "we built X, it does Y, here's where it fails."
- **Crossposting Substack copy with no LW framing.** Per the LW FAQ: this signals "doesn't care about LW culture." Rewrite for the audience or don't post.
- **Hidden agenda.** If readers smell that the post is product-marketing dressed as a technical writeup, they will say so in comments and karma will tank. Eliezer's rule: spell out the ulterior motive.
- **AI-generated copy in the post itself.** The 2025 LLM policy debate makes this radioactive in 2026. A post *about* an AI-generated map can succeed; a post *written by* an LLM about that map will not.
- **Defending in the comments instead of updating.** When critics show up, the prestige move is "good catch, here's what we're changing" — not justification. Ozzie Gooen's Squiggle launches are the model. Alexei's Arbital postmortem hit 233 karma *because* it was honest about failure.
- **Front-loading the homepage link.** LW readers click in-post links if the post earned the click. A post whose entire payload is "go try our site" reads as bait.
- **Politicized examples.** Don't use abortion, Trump, Israel/Palestine, etc. as your demo topics on LW even though those are the topics where argument mapping shines most. Use AI alignment cruxes, EA cause prioritization disagreements, or something rationalist-flavored.
- **Vague "community" framing.** Posts saying "we want to build a community of rational debaters" die instantly. Post artifacts, not aspirations.
- **Top-level pitching as a brand-new account.** New users with no prior comments posting "I built a tool" go to manual mod review and often Personal. Build at least a few comments of context first (could be done in days).
- **Looking like Kialo.** That sterile pro/con tree aesthetic is pre-rejected. Argumend's parchment/serif look helps, but the *structure* matters more — emphasize cruxes and confidence-weighted evidence, not pro/con counts.
