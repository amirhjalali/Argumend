# Distribution Channel: The Rationalist / TPOT / Post-Rat / e/acc X Scene

**Date:** 2026-04-28
**Analyst:** Research agent 05/10 in Argumend distribution swarm
**Scope:** "This Part of Twitter" and adjacent rat / post-rat / EA / AI-safety / e/acc orbits as a distribution channel for Argumend.

## 1. Snapshot of the scene April 2026

The X subgraph relevant to Argumend is not one community — it is six overlapping circles that mostly hate each other in public and reply-guy each other in DMs. Mapping them honestly is the first job, because they share gateway accounts but they ratio different things.

**Rat-Twitter (the LessWrong-adjacent core).** The original SSC/LessWrong/CFAR-shaped audience that migrated to Twitter around 2018-2020. Anchored by Eliezer Yudkowsky (@ESYudkowsky), Scott Alexander (@slatestarcodex), Robin Hanson (@robinhanson), Zvi Mowshowitz, the AI safety researcher diaspora, and prediction-market people (Manifold, Metaculus). They post about decision theory, AI x-risk, Bayesian epistemics, COVID forecasts, ethics. **This is the audience whose epistemics most closely match Argumend's product premise.** ([Wikipedia: Rationalist community](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rationalist_community))

**TPOT / post-rat (the vibes-pilled splinter).** "This Part of Twitter" — a label coined around 2021-2022 for a loose constellation of mutuals who used to be rationalists or EA-curious but got disillusioned with what they perceived as cult-like dogma and lack of attention to spirituality, embodiment, and meaning ([New Atlantis: Rational Magic](https://www.thenewatlantis.com/publications/rational-magic), [LessWrong: Explaining the Twitter Postrat Scene](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/rtM3jFaoQn3eoAiPh/explaining-the-twitter-postrat-scene)). Aesthetic markers: Jungian archetypes, ritual, occultism, polyamory, group houses, Vibecamp. Anchored by @VividVoid_, @nosilverv, @QC_Lit, @eigenrobot, @nwthrowaway2 (@nwtb_), Sarah Perry. Consolidated during COVID; its existence is now profiled in Know Your Meme ([KYM: TPOT/PostRat](https://knowyourmeme.com/memes/subcultures/tpot-postrat)).

**e/acc (effective accelerationism).** Started May 2022 in a pseudonymous newsletter by @BasedBeffJezos, @bayeslord, @zestular, and @creatine_cycle ([Wikipedia: e/acc](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Effective_accelerationism)). Beff Jezos was unmasked by Forbes in December 2023 as Guillaume Verdon, a former Google quantum engineer. Now sprawls across a more VC-and-founder demographic: Marc Andreessen (@pmarca, ~1.8M followers per [xstalk profile](https://xstalk.com/profile/pmarca)), Garry Tan, Aaron Levie, Mark Pincus all explicitly added "e/acc" to their bios after the November 2024 election. Posts: techno-optimism, "deregulate everything," explicit hostility to AI doomers.

**EA-Twitter / AI-safety Twitter.** Overlaps with rat-Twitter but is more institutional — Anthropic, METR, ARC, AI Safety Institute people; Open Phil program officers; 80,000 Hours. Less meme-y. PauseAI (@PauseAI) is the activist wing — they organized 13-country protests in May 2024 around the AI Seoul Summit and a London protest of ~500 people ([PauseAI](https://pauseai.info/2024-may), [Wikipedia: PauseAI](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/PauseAI)).

**"ngmi" / tech-bro adjacent.** The crypto-twitter-flavored layer that uses TPOT vocabulary (cope, ngmi, ratioed, mid) but isn't really part of the epistemic project — it's a shared dialect more than a shared community.

**The big-tent gateway accounts who span camps.** @sama (4.7M followers, [foxbusiness profile](https://www.foxbusiness.com/technology/openai-ceo-sam-altman-rings-2025-cryptic-concerning-poem-ais-future)), @paulg, @patio11, @balajis, @PalmerLuckey, @michael_nielsen, @karpathy, @swyx — these accounts are read by everyone in every camp regardless of faction. **Getting an RT from one of them is the single highest-leverage event possible in this scene.**

The thing to understand: the camps are politically incompatible (PauseAI vs e/acc on AI; rat-Twitter vs post-rat on epistemics; EA vs everyone on charity), but they share three things: a tolerance for long-form arguments, a fluency in probabilistic and game-theoretic vocabulary, and the same handful of gateway accounts.

## 3. How content spreads in this scene

The mechanics are different from mass-market Twitter, and Argumend's pitch should be tuned to them.

**RT chains via gateway accounts.** The standard pattern: a sub-10K-follower account (the original poster) drops a thread. A second-ring account (10K-50K) quote-tweets with one editorial sentence. A gateway account (>200K — Yudkowsky, Patel, Hanson, Tan, Andreessen) RTs *that* QT. The original poster gets ~80% of their views from the gateway RT, not from the QT chain. **Implication: target the QT-chain accounts, not the gateways directly.** ([LessWrong: Explaining the Twitter Postrat Scene](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/rtM3jFaoQn3eoAiPh/explaining-the-twitter-postrat-scene) describes this as "community retweets as a filter surfacing the best nuggets from the rest of Twitter.")

**Quote-tweets as the load-bearing format.** This scene almost never does pure RTs; everything gets a one-line editorial gloss. That gloss is where the ratio battle happens. A QT that says "this is exactly right" performs differently than "this is interesting but..."

**Group DMs / Signal / Discord backchannels.** Per [New Atlantis](https://www.thenewatlantis.com/publications/rational-magic), the post-rat scene is organized through "a backchannel network of private group chats and Discord servers and Zoom rooms" more than through public Twitter. Most viral threads were *workshopped in DMs first*. You cannot reach this layer without warm intros — which means the right move is to seed one or two warm relationships before launching anything.

**Spaces.** Less load-bearing than DMs, but a 2-hour Space with the right co-host (e.g., Liron Shapira, Robin Hanson, Aella) is the single most concentrated event-format for this audience.

**Vibecamp.** In-person meetup, originally annual. Vibecamp 4 ran June 19-22, 2025 ([Vibecamp 4 FAQ](https://vibe.camp/vibecamp-4-faq/)). Aella + Robin Hanson hosted a session at Vibecamp 2023. **Sponsoring or speaking at Vibecamp is one of the most efficient ways to convert from "X follower" to "warm relationship" in the TPOT layer.**

**Substack cross-pollination.** ACX, Zvi, Dwarkesh, and Vivid Void all link out to one another's work. A Substack post that an Argumend map is embedded in spreads via Substack Notes too, not just X.

**Hostile QT as accelerant.** If you get QT'd hostilely by @pmarca or @ESYudkowsky, that's *good*. The Liron-Hanson debate cycle generated more reach for Liron from Hanson's skeptical replies than from any sympathetic RT. The asymmetry: rage-engagement converts to followers in this scene because the audience self-selects toward "I want to understand the argument."

## 4. Past product launches via X — case studies

**Roam Research (2019-2020).** The canonical rat-Twitter launch story. Per [productidentity.co](https://productidentity.co/p/4-roam-research-what-comes-after) and [Indie Hackers](https://www.indiehackers.com/post/roam-research-reaches-1m-arr-in-6-weeks-20faee74e6), Roam reached **$1M ARR within 6 weeks** of paid launch, fueled by adoption from "20 mathematicians adjacent to the Rationalist Community and LessWrong" plus EA, OpenAI, DeepMind, Google Brain, and Ribbonfarm community early users. The **#roamcult** hashtag was the viral mouthpiece — users posted screenshots of their bidirectional-link "scripts" and tagged Conor White-Sullivan. **Lesson: a piece of UGC users want to show off > a feature list.**

**Manifold Markets (Dec 2021-).** Built explicitly to be "Twitter for prediction markets." Per [Manifold's site](https://manifold.markets/about) and the [LessWrong launch announcement](https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/qcYu6fMb6qg4zcftn/create-a-prediction-market-in-two-minutes-on-manifold), the founders (Austin Chen, James + Stephen Grugett) launched directly into the EA Forum and LessWrong, *then* let the markets get embedded in tweets. The wedge feature was "create a market in 2 minutes, share a link, friends bet" — frictionless link-sharing on X was the whole growth loop.

**Metaculus.** Slower, more institutional. Per the [Metaculus blog](https://www.metaculus.com/), the **2025 ACX series** with Scott Alexander became Metaculus's fastest-growing series ever — 3000+ forecasters, 2x the prior year. The **Bridgewater x Metaculus contest** registered 17,000+ competitors, 10x prior. **Lesson: the channel that 10x'd Metaculus was *one Scott Alexander Substack post*.** That is the single highest-leverage public surface for Argumend.

**Lex.page (Oct 2022).** Per [Every: How Lex Happened](https://every.to/divinations/how-lex-happened), Nathan Baschez's launch tweet drove **25,000 signups in 24 hours**. Format: short tweet + GIF of the AI completion in action + "+++" command demo. Then Baschez seeded follow-up threads with specific use cases (Lex helped me write this essay). **Lesson: a 6-second product demo GIF beats a 20-tweet thread for cold acquisition. Once people are on the site, the thread can do retention work.**

**PauseAI (May 2023-).** Per [PauseAI's own site](https://pauseai.info/2024-may), the May 2024 international protest — 13 countries simultaneously — was coordinated through X plus Discord. The X playbook: each city's local organizer posted a thread before the event with the address, hashtag, photo target. Result: ~500 in London, ~5K total. **Lesson for Argumend: even non-product launches benefit from a "physical event + X coordination" combo.**

**The non-pattern.** Argdown (HN front page Aug 2024 — [HN 41186310](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=41186310)), PredictionBook, Arbital, Ought's Elicit. Each had champions in the rat community but no QT-chain mechanic. **Negative lesson: being respected by rat-Twitter ≠ being shared by rat-Twitter.** Sharing requires a hook, an asset, or a fight.

## 5. Etiquette / what gets you ratio'd

**What flops:**
- "I built a thing!" with a marketing landing page link as tweet 1. Reads as out-group. Zero RTs.
- Hashtags. The scene has zero tolerance for them. "#AItools" is suicide.
- Verified-blue corporate-account voice ("We're excited to announce…"). The scene reads "we" as bot.
- Asking for RTs. Fatal.
- Pure positive testimonials. Reads as astroturf. The scene trusts criticism more than praise.
- Single-image promo cards with logos. The scene strongly prefers screenshots, raw text, or short-loop GIFs.

**What lands:**
- **Long, opinionated thread + free demo link buried late in the thread.** Tweet 1 hooks with a claim or a contrarian take. Tweets 2-7 develop the argument. Tweets 8-9 introduce the tool *as receipts* for the claim, not as the point. This is the canonical Lex / Manifold / Dwarkesh pattern.
- **Self-deprecation.** "Built this in a weekend, probably broken, would love to be told why" performs ~3x "Excited to launch."
- **Picking a fight with a known position.** A thread saying "Yudkowsky's argument for AI doom is wrong because [crux 1] [crux 2] [crux 3]" with an Argumend map will get more reach than any neutral framing. The scene rewards taking a side.
- **Receipts.** Linking specific past tweets, papers, or arguments as evidence. This scene is uniquely receptive to footnote-density.
- **The "I was wrong" thread.** "I used to think X. Then I read Y and built this map and now I think Z" — converts at unusual rates.

**What ratios you (and how to recover):** Per [BuzzVoice's ratio explainer](https://buzzvoice.com/blog/what-does-ratio-mean-on-x/), a ratio in this scene specifically signals "the audience disagrees with you so strongly the replies outweigh the likes." Recoverable by: (a) replying with a steelman of the strongest critique, (b) shipping a fix in <24 hours, (c) admitting you were wrong on a specific sub-claim. **Doubling down on a ratio'd take in this scene is reputationally fatal.**

## 6. Concrete actions for Argumend

Ranked by leverage / cost.

**A1 — [HIGHEST LEVERAGE] One thread analyzing a live AI-doom-vs-accelerationist debate, Argumend map embedded as the 9th tweet, pitched warm to @liron and @robinhanson.**
The Liron Shapira / Robin Hanson 2024 doom debate is the canonical recent artifact, with public LessWrong + YouTube + tweet records ([Liron writeup](https://lironshapira.substack.com/p/robin-hanson-ai-doom-debate)). Argumend can ingest the debate, generate a crux map showing the 4-5 actual disagreements (FOOM speed, alignment difficulty, deceptive mesa-optimization, base rates, etc.), and post a thread that says: "Here are the 5 actual cruxes between the doomer and the gradualist position — most public AI debates argue past these without naming them." Pitch warm to @liron *first* (he runs Doom Debates and will engage), let him QT, then his QT travels to Hanson, Yudkowsky, and the AI-safety researcher cluster organically.

**A2 — Ship one Argumend map of a Dwarkesh Patel interview within 24 hours of release.**
Dwarkesh drops a new interview every 1-2 weeks ([dwarkesh.com](https://www.dwarkesh.com/)). Each is 2-3 hours of dense argument. There is consistent demand from his audience for "key cruxes" / "tldr" content. Argumend's job: be the first to ship a high-quality crux map of every Dwarkesh episode within 24 hours. Tag @dwarkesh_sp once. Don't beg for the RT — ship the second map, and the third, and let the pattern get noticed. **This is a six-month commitment with a step-function payoff: when Dwarkesh QTs the map, Argumend gets ~150K relevant impressions.**

**A3 — Get Scott Alexander to mention Argumend in an ACX post.**
Per the Metaculus precedent ("ACX 2025 forecasting series 10x'd traffic"), one ACX mention is the single highest-leverage public surface in this orbit. Cost-of-acquisition: ship one feature he asks for. Path: send Scott a DM (he reads them) with a working Argumend map of *one of his own essays* (e.g., "The Categories Were Made for Man, Not Man for the Categories"), say "if this is useful, take it; if not, throw it out." Don't pitch. Don't ask. Let the gift be the pitch.

**A4 — Sponsor or speak at Vibecamp 5 (likely summer 2026).**
Vibecamp is the in-person convergence point for the TPOT layer ([Vibecamp 4 FAQ](https://vibe.camp/vibecamp-4-faq/)). Run a "build your map" workshop. Cost: probably <$5K sponsorship + travel. ROI: ~50 warm DMs.

**A5 — Build the "Argumend bot" pattern.**
A reply-bot summoned by `@argumend` that posts a one-image crux map of the parent thread. Additive (others brag about summoning it), low-friction (no signup), demonstrable (the bot's output is the ad). Grok-summon and "remind me" bots prove the format works.

**A6 — Pick one e/acc-vs-doomer fight and don't stay neutral.**
The scene punishes mealy-mouth. Ship one map that takes a position — e.g., "the doomer side is right that alignment is unsolved but wrong about timelines" — and let both sides argue *in the replies*. The thread becomes a live demo of crux extraction; the replies *fill out the map*.

## 7. Watch-outs

**Political mines.** This scene contains active fascists (@zerohplovecraft, Curtis Yarvin), active accused predators (chaosprime was outed as a child molester per [theasterisk.substack.com](https://theasterisk.substack.com/p/reflecting-on-a-few-very-very-strange)), and an actual splinter cult — the Zizians, suspected in four 2025 murders ([Wikipedia: Rationalist community](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rationalist_community)). **Argumend cannot afford to be RT'd by certain accounts. Maintain a bright-line block list before launching anything. Specifically: any Argumend-branded asset that ends up RT'd by Yarvin / NRx accounts will permanently torch Kialo-style EDU and institutional channels.**

**Intra-faction wars.** Choosing to sponsor Vibecamp signals "TPOT-aligned" and may alienate the harder-core LessWrong/MIRI faction (which considers TPOT a degeneration). Choosing to pitch to PauseAI signals "doomer" and instantly burns e/acc bridges. **Mitigation: run two parallel threads of outreach — one rat-/AI-safety-flavored, one e/acc-flavored — and never cross-tag the gateway accounts of opposing camps in the same tweet.**

**Rage-engagement vs RT-engagement.** A thread that gets 5K likes from sympathizers + 200 angry QTs is healthy. A thread that gets 200 likes + 5K angry QTs is being dragged. Distinguish by reading the QTs *before* celebrating. A "mid-AI" or "midwit" QT chain from e/acc accounts can torch credibility faster than the QTs add followers.

**The "I built a thing" failure mode is self-inflicted.** Argumend has shipped multiple things and gotten near-zero traction (per founder context: "many plans shipped, no real users"). The reason is almost certainly that previous launches read as launches, not as arguments. **The scene shares arguments. It does not share products.** Every Argumend public post should be primarily an argument, secondarily a tool demo.

**Verification trap.** Buying X premium / Verified makes Argumend's account look corporate-bot to this scene. Better: have the *founder's personal account* post, no logo, no blue check, just `argumend.org` in bio. The scene shares people, not brands.

**Do not feed the AI ethics culture-war.** Race / gender / LLM-bias topics are mines that this scene will mostly ignore but *quote-tweets from outside the scene* can land Argumend in a Twitter Files-style narrative. Stay on epistemic-mechanics territory.
