## 1. Live-Debate State — April 2026

### 1a. Psychedelics Policy — BREAKOUT HOT

April 2026 is a genuine inflection point for psychedelics policy:

- **April 18, 2026:** President Trump signed an executive order directing the FDA to **prioritize review** of psychedelic compounds and the DEA to reduce research restrictions ([Harvard Petrie-Flom Center, April 18](https://petrieflom.law.harvard.edu/2026/04/18/a-new-executive-order-on-psychedelics-q-a-with-i-glenn-cohen-and-mason-marks/)).
- **April 24, 2026:** FDA issued **national priority vouchers** to three companies studying psilocybin (treatment-resistant depression, major depressive disorder) and methylone (PTSD) ([CNBC, April 24](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/04/24/fda-psychedelic-drug-research-trump.html); [Axios, April 24](https://www.axios.com/2026/04/24/psychedelic-research-trump-fda-vouchers); [STAT News, April 24](https://www.statnews.com/2026/04/24/trump-psychedelic-drugs-fda-priority-review-mental-health-treatment/); [CNN, April 24](https://www.cnn.com/2026/04/24/health/fda-psychedelic-drugs-priority-vouchers)).
- Compass Pathways' COMP360 (psilocybin for treatment-resistant depression) is approaching FDA approval with Phase 3 results.
- *PharmaVoice*: "How does the FDA really feel about psychedelics?" — noting tension between RFK Jr.'s HHS and the FDA's traditional caution ([PharmaVoice](https://www.pharmavoice.com/news/fda-rfk-makary-compass-psilocybin-drug-approval/812697/)).
- State-level: Oregon Measure 109 (therapeutic psilocybin) is operational; Colorado's Proposition 122 in implementation. The debate has shifted from "should we legalize?" to "how do we regulate the therapeutic pathway?"

### 1b. Longevity / Anti-Aging — STEADY-BUILDING

- **April 23, 2026:** FDA cleared the first **cellular rejuvenation trial** — a landmark event. The longevity market is now a $120 billion market per GlobeNewswire ([GlobeNewswire, April 23](https://www.globenewswire.com/news-release/2026/04/23/3279692/0/en/The-Longevity-Trade-Is-No-Longer-a-Buzzword-It-s-a-120-Billion-Market-and-the-FDA-Just-Cleared-the-First-Cellular-Rejuvenation-Trial.html)).
- The **GLP-1 longevity debate** is the hottest sub-crux: Nature Biotechnology asks "Are GLP-1s the first longevity drugs?" ([Nature Biotech, 2025](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41587-025-02932-1)). Harvard Science Review (February 2026) covers "GLP-1 aftermath: muscle loss and cellular aging" ([Harvard Science Review, Feb 23 2026](https://harvardsciencereview.org/2026/02/23/the-glp-1-aftermath-what-the-science-says-about-muscle-loss-and-cellular-aging/)).
- The 2026 Longevity Science Forum in San Diego reflects the growing institutionalization of the field.
- The muscle loss controversy: up to 40% of GLP-1 weight loss is lean body mass — a safety concern for longevity applications in older adults.
- FDA regulatory gap: aging is not recognized as a disease indication; no regulatory pathway for anti-aging interventions. This creates a crux: should the FDA create one?

### 1c. Gene Editing / Bioethics — MODERATE

- CRISPR-based somatic gene therapies are moving through FDA approval; the human germline editing debate is less live than 2019 (post-He Jiankui) but simmers in bioethics journals.
- The primary live debate is about **embryo selection** (preimplantation genetic testing for complex traits like IQ or disease risk) rather than germline editing. As polygenic scores improve, this is becoming operationally possible.
- No major breaking news events in April 2026 specific to gene editing (as of research date).

### 1d. Animal Welfare — LOW-ENERGY

- No major April 2026 breaking news events. Cultivated meat regulatory progress is slow post-USDA approval complexities. EA-driven campaigns continue but at reduced visibility.
- The most active sub-debate: welfare standards for farmed fish and invertebrates (shrimp, lobster) — where scientific evidence of sentience is advancing.

### 1e. Effective Altruism Discourse — RECOVERING

- EA is in a multi-year credibility repair process post-FTX (Sam Bankman-Fried's fraud conviction). The community is smaller but arguably more intellectually serious.
- The internal EA debate — **AI safety vs. global health / animal welfare** — has intensified as AI capabilities have accelerated. Is it rational to prioritize long-run x-risk when near-term suffering is happening now? The DALY vs. expected value crux.
- LessWrong and the EA Forum remain the primary hubs. The EA Forum (forum.effectivealtruism.org) is unusually high-quality for structured argument and crux-identification — it is the closest thing to Argumend that exists in textual form.
- Key critics: *The Guardian*'s coverage of EA ethics; Émile Torres and Timnit Gebru's "TESCREAL" critique of EA-adjacent ideology.

## 2. Top 5 Cruxes (Across Sub-topics)

**1. Should psychedelic-assisted therapies be approved through standard FDA pathways or accelerated mechanisms, and what are the risks of premature approval?**  
The active crux: Compass Pathways' Phase 3 results are positive, but critics (including some FDA advisors) worry about trial design (blinding is impossible), durability of effects, and integration therapy requirements. The FDA's April 24 priority vouchers signal acceleration — but at what quality-of-evidence threshold?

**2. Are GLP-1 drugs (Ozempic/Wegovy) the first genuine longevity therapeutics, or is the longevity framing premature hype?**  
Nature Biotechnology frames GLP-1s as potential longevity drugs based on multi-organ aging reversal in animal models and cardiovascular event reduction in humans. Skeptics note: (a) no long-term human longevity data, (b) muscle loss risk, (c) drugs not studied in healthy-aging populations, (d) FDA doesn't recognize aging as an indication. Clean empirical crux with strong researchers on both sides.

**3. Should preimplantation genetic testing for polygenic traits (intelligence, disease risk, behavioral dispositions) be permitted, and under what regulatory framework?**  
As polygenic scoring becomes powerful enough to have meaningful predictive validity, the ethics of embryo selection move from theoretical to operational. Disability rights advocates, religious groups, and some bioethicists argue this constitutes eugenics. Proponents argue it is an extension of existing reproductive autonomy. Live academic debate; politically incipient.

**4. Within EA: should AI safety / long-run x-risk dominate resource allocation, or does expected value calculation fail to justify neglecting near-term suffering?**  
The internal EA crux that determines billions in philanthropic allocation. "Longtermists" (Nick Beckstead, Will MacAskill) argue that astronomically large potential futures justify prioritizing AI/x-risk. Critics (including some within EA: Holden Karnofsky has partially shifted) argue the expected value reasoning is too uncertain to guide action away from near-term tractable interventions.

**5. Does the moral weight of animal suffering justify major dietary and economic disruption, and can it be quantified?**  
The animal welfare / EA crux on moral patient status and suffering quantification. If invertebrate sentience is confirmed, the scale of factory farming-related suffering is staggering by any utilitarian accounting. But the empirical evidence on invertebrate consciousness is genuinely uncertain, and the policy interventions required are economically massive.
