# Argumend 14-Day Observation Protocol

**Date:** 2026-04-09
**Purpose:** Replace iteration-on-guesses with iteration-on-observed-behavior. Pair document to `2026-04-09-product-direction-memo.md`.

## Rule 0 — No code for 14 days

No merges. No worktree agents. No "quick SEO fix." No new topics. No blog posts. No schema tweaks. If you touch the repo in the next 14 days, you are back in orbit.

The cost of doing nothing for 14 days is approximately zero. The cost of another quarter of orbit is another quarter of orbit.

## Recruitment (20 people, biased toward NOT you)

- **5 people who argue on the internet regularly but have never heard of rationalism.** Reddit debaters, Twitter repliers, group-chat opinion-havers.
- **5 people who read Substacks but not ACX/Asterisk.** "Smart newsletter readers" who are not in the LessWrong orbit.
- **5 high school or college students.** Age 16–21. Bonus if they're in a debate club or take history/civics seriously.
- **5 people who have had a real bad argument with a family member in the last 6 months about something contested.** This is the "mend" target audience.

**Not allowed:**
- Your tech friends
- Anyone on your Twitter follow list
- Anyone who has already seen Argumend
- Anyone who knows the word "steelman" before you say it

## Format: 5 watched + 15 unwatched

### 5 watched sessions — 20 minutes each

Sit next to them (or screenshare). Give them exactly one sentence and nothing else:

> *"This is a tool called Argumend. Take a look."*

- Do not explain what it is.
- Do not defend anything they criticize.
- Do not clarify when they look confused.
- Do not ask them what they think until the very end.
- Stay silent unless they ask a direct question. If they do, answer in one sentence and shut up again.

Take notes in real time. Record: where their eyes go first, where they click first, where they get stuck, where they check out, when they smile, when they frown, what they say out loud.

At the 20-minute mark, ask only one question: *"What did you think this was for?"* Write their answer down verbatim.

### 15 unwatched sessions — link + followup

Send the link with the same sentence: *"This is a tool called Argumend. Take a look."*

24 hours later, send one followup message:
> *"Did you go back? What did you think it was for?"*

Record whatever they say verbatim. If they say "haven't had time," that is data — write it down too.

## What to record — three things per person

One page per person. No more. Format:

```
## Person #N
- Name:
- Age / category:
- Date:
- Session type: watched / unwatched

Under-10-second question:
(What did they say the product was, before you told them?
If they couldn't name it in 10 seconds, write "[silence for X seconds]")

Day-2 return:
(Did they come back the next day unprompted? Yes / No.
No judgment. Don't nudge them.)

"It's for ____" sentence:
(In their OWN words. Don't correct. Don't clarify.
Write it down even if it's wrong.)

One direct quote:
("___")

My surprise (one line):
(What did I expect to happen that didn't, or vice versa?)
```

## What NOT to do

- **Do not ask "what would make this better?"** Users are terrible at this. It produces feature requests disguised as insight. You want observed behavior, not wishlists.
- **Do not explain the methodology.** If they don't understand "crux" or "confidence score," that is the finding.
- **Do not pitch the vision.** The vision is a lagging indicator if the product doesn't speak for itself.
- **Do not count clicks or run analytics.** Qualitative > quantitative at this stage. 20 notebook pages beat a Mixpanel dashboard.
- **Do not talk to 20 similar people.** Deliberately mix the categories above. Homogeneous samples will confirm whatever prior you already hold.

## What comes back to the next session

1. **The 20 notebook pages** (or a pasted summary by category)
2. **Your gut feeling:** Did you pattern-match to one of Directions A / B / C from the product-direction memo? Or something you didn't see before?
3. **One answer:** *"What surprised me most?"* One sentence.

Then we brainstorm the actual direction from a notebook grounded in observed behavior, instead of another plan stacked on another guess.

## Success criteria

The 14 days succeed if you can answer these questions at the end:

- [ ] Can a stranger name what Argumend is in under 10 seconds?
- [ ] Does anyone ever come back on day 2 without being asked?
- [ ] Is there one word or one phrase that multiple strangers used to describe it, unprompted?
- [ ] Did any of the 20 people experience a moment that looked emotional — surprise, recognition, softening?
- [ ] Is there a pattern by category (students vs family-arguers vs substack readers) that suggests one audience is the actual user?

If you can answer those five questions, the next direction decision will be grounded. If you can't, the 14 days weren't enough and we extend.

## Failure mode to watch for

If on day 6 you find yourself opening the repo "just to fix one thing" — that's the orbit reasserting itself. Close the laptop. Go outside. The 14 days are the whole point.
