## 2. Adversarial Review

### 2A. False-balance check on settled science (per directive) — PASS, with one caveat

The map does **not** false-balance vaccine safety. Specifically:
- The vaccine pillar's `skeptic_premise` (which here = the *pro-vaccine / anti-MAHA* establishment side) flatly states schedule safety is "settled by decades of evidence" and leads with the IOM 2011/2013 reviews and VSD — the actual scientific consensus is stated as consensus, not as one side of a 50/50.
- The MAHA-favorable vaccine item (`aluminum-asthma-study`) is **carefully fenced**: coded as motivating "further research," explicitly noted as observational/confounded/unreplicated, replicability scored 5. It is not presented as evidence the schedule is harmful.
- The proponent rebuttal's strongest claim — "no large modern RCT of full schedule vs lighter schedule" — is **directly answered** by the 2013 IOM report item, which examined exactly the whole-schedule question. The map does not leave the "untested schedule" claim standing unrebutted.
- The fabricated-citations item further undercuts MAHA rigor on the vaccine side.

**Caveat / residual risk:** The meta-claim and `crux` framing ("whole-schedule comparative trial," verification_status `impossible`) could be *read* as conceding that schedule-wide safety is genuinely open pending an impossible trial. The crux text mitigates this by noting the observational/quasi-experimental evidence is the consensus answer, but a careless reader could take "impossible to verify" as "unknown." **Recommend tightening the crux description to state explicitly that the absence of an RCT does not imply the safety question is unresolved — the surveillance evidence is already decisive.** This is the one place the structure flirts with false balance without crossing into it.

The vaccine *autism* link — the single most settled "MAHA gets it wrong" point — is **not addressed anywhere.** Given RFK Jr's history, an evidence item explicitly noting the thoroughly-debunked MMR-autism claim (and that MAHA does not officially rest on it) would harden the map against false-balance criticism. Currently a notable omission. (See fixes.)

### 2B. General balance

The map is well-constructed and correctly identifies that MAHA's ground varies by pillar: **strong on food/GRAS, genuinely contested on fluoride at 0.7 mg/L, weak-to-discredited on vaccine restructuring.** This differentiated treatment is exactly right and is the map's biggest strength.

**Strongest version of each side:**
- *Vaccine* — establishment side is strong and correctly dominant. MAHA's *best* legitimate point (full-schedule combined regimen rests on observational data + committee judgment, which is technically true) is fairly stated, then properly bounded.
- *Food* — MAHA's strongest pillar; Hall + GRAS are real. Skeptic's best point (NOVA is coarse; Hall himself cautions over-extrapolation) is well-represented.
- *Fluoride* — genuinely the most balanced; the 0.7 vs 1.5 mg/L gap is the real crux and honestly drawn.
- *Institutions* — the Prasad/Makary internal-contradiction is a sharp, fair point that capture-rhetoric coexists with deregulation.

**Weak / overstated:** none materially overstated in MAHA's favor — if anything the map is appropriately skeptical. Two internal **date/URL mismatches** (`cdc-walks-back-fluoride`, `internal-contradiction-prasad-makary`) are the main integrity defects.
