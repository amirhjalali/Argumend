## 2. Adversarial Review

**Overall balance:** The meta-claim is pro-DOGE ("eliminated waste… leaner and more effective"), and the map again loads the strongest evidence against it (savings reconciliation, IRS revenue loss, Alsup ruling, First Circuit, rehiring pattern). But unlike the tariffs map, the *for* side here gets several genuinely strong, well-chosen items — GAO improper-payments (real waste exists), the 99.8% performance-review anomaly, the SCOTUS stay, and *Trump v. Slaughter*. The pro-reform-diagnosis / anti-DOGE-execution distinction is drawn well (especially via Pahlka), which is the intellectually correct frame.

**Strongest version of each side, per pillar:**
- **Savings** — *Against* (verified $2B vs claimed $175B; IRS −$861B) is devastating and correct. *For*'s best legitimate point — that the *existence* of a large improper-payments pool justified the attempt — is fairly given via GAO. Balanced.
- **Service disruption** — *Against* dominates (rehiring pattern is the killer illustration). *For* (self-correction / congressional checks) is steelmanned. The strongest *for* point missing: any **realized, verified savings or efficiency gain** (e.g., a specific duplicative-program elimination that stuck) — the for side is almost entirely "the system contained the harm," never "here is a concrete win." This is a real balance gap.
- **Legality** — Genuinely contested; unitary-executive theory + SCOTUS stay + Slaughter (for) vs Alsup + First Circuit + ICA/Train (against). Best-balanced pillar; the normative-vs-precedent framing of the crux is apt.
- **Reform vs capacity** — Pahlka/Kettl (against) vs the 99.8% anomaly (for) is a fair, high-quality contest.

**Weak / overstated claims & sourcing defects (the main issues):**
- **Source-quality is the map's weakest dimension.** Several load-bearing or supporting items lean on low-authority outlets: `ssa-service-collapse` → **Moneywise**; `congress-court-checks` → **GearJunkie**; `gao-improper-payments` → a third-party fact-check site rather than GAO directly. The underlying facts are likely fine, but for a contested political topic these citations are easy to attack.
- **Causal attribution to DOGE** is asserted more than isolated in the service-disruption pillar (SSA backlogs, VA waits predate or coincide with broader trends). The `reasoning` fields gesture at this but the methodology/crux is where it's properly handled.
- No internal date/URL mismatches detected (cleaner than the Ukraine/RFK maps on that axis).

**Missing cruxes/evidence:**
- No concrete **verified-savings win** on the for side (noted above).
- No item on **DOGE's data-access/privacy controversies** (Treasury payment system, SSA/IRS data) — a major strand of the real debate entirely absent; arguably belongs in the legality pillar.

## 3. Prioritized Fixes (top 5)

1. **Upgrade low-authority sources:** replace **Moneywise** (`ssa-service-collapse`) and **GearJunkie** (`congress-court-checks`) with GAO/SSA/WaPo/appropriations primaries, and cite **GAO directly** in `gao-improper-payments` instead of factually.co. Biggest credibility win.
2. **Verify the post-cutoff legal/quant anchors:** First Circuit March 2026 affirmance, Alsup Sept 12 2025 SJ specifics, Yale $861B / 31% IRS figures, and the ~25,000 rehire aggregate. All NEEDS LIVE CHECK and load-bearing.
3. **Sharpen DOGE-causation caveats** in the service-disruption items (SSA, VA) — explicitly note pre-existing trends and the VA's attrition-vs-firing framing so attribution isn't overstated.
4. **Add a concrete "for" evidence item of a verified, durable efficiency win** (a specific eliminated-duplication or recovered-fraud case that survived reconciliation) so the proponent side isn't limited to "the system contained the harm."
5. **Consider adding a DOGE data-access/privacy item** to the legality pillar — a significant real-world dimension currently missing entirely.
