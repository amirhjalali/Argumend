# Review: open-weight-ai-models

**Topic:** "Releasing Open-Weight Frontier AI Models"
**Reviewer note:** This map mixes durable, verifiable ML/policy sources (NTIA report, LLaMA 2023 leak, arXiv papers, AI Action Plan July 2025) with 2026 capability claims (DeepSeek V4-Pro, Qwen 3.6, April 2026 benchmarks) that postdate the cutoff → **NEEDS LIVE CHECK** for the latter. Reviewer note: several closed-frontier comparison figures (e.g., "Claude 4.5 Opus 80.9 SWE-bench") are speculative future-model claims and should be treated as illustrative, not load-bearing.

---

## 1. Source / Claim Verification

### Pillar `marginal-uplift`

- **`tamper-resistant-safeguards-fail`** — Claim: refusal training reversible for a few hundred dollars of fine-tuning; per "Tamper-Resistant Safeguards" (arXiv 2408.00761) and "On Evaluating the Durability of Safeguards" (arXiv 2412.07097). **VERIFIABLE.** Both arXiv IDs are real, correctly-identified 2024 papers; the finding that current safeguards survive only weak attacks and that fine-tuning can strip refusal cheaply is accurately summarized. **Subtle point:** 2412.07097 ("On Evaluating the Durability of Safeguards") is actually a *critique* of the durability claims in 2408.00761 (it argues the original eval overstated robustness) — pairing them as if jointly supporting "guardrails removable" is roughly right in direction but the two are partly in tension. Verify the citation pairing reflects this nuance. Weight 8/8/9/9 reasonable. Correctly coded "for."
- **`biorisk-evals-weak-signal`** — Claim: Epoch AI review — evals "do not provide strong evidence that LLMs can enable amateurs to develop bioweapons"; bottleneck is tacit lab skill/materials; author concedes evals weak in both directions. **VERIFIABLE / accurate.** Epoch AI Gradient Updates published exactly this; the "weak signal in both directions" caveat is correctly included (prevents over-reading absence-of-evidence as evidence-of-absence). RAND RRA3797-1 as co-source is plausible. Epoch URL well-formed. Strong, balanced "against" item. Good.
- **`elicitation-attack`** — Claim: Jan 2026 "elicitation attacks" use a safeguarded closed model to uplift an open model without harmful prompts, collapsing "the closed model already knows this." **NEEDS LIVE CHECK.** Source is a LessWrong "Paper Highlights of January 2026" roundup — appropriately scored low (6/6/6) as a synthesis not a primary result. The mechanism described is conceptually coherent. Verify the underlying papers exist; consider citing them directly rather than the roundup.
- **`open-model-capability-trajectory`** — Claim: DeepSeek V4-Pro (1.6T total/49B active, ~Apache-2.0) ~3,206 Codeforces; Qwen3.6-27B 77.2 SWE-bench vs Claude 4.5 Opus 80.9. **NEEDS LIVE CHECK — and partly speculative.** "Claude 4.5 Opus" and these exact April 2026 benchmark numbers are future/post-cutoff and likely illustrative. The map honestly scores directness 6 and notes coding ≠ catastrophic uplift. Treat all specific 2026 benchmark figures as unverified. MIT Tech Review URL well-formed as a slug.

## 2. Adversarial Review

**Overall balance:** The most epistemically careful of the five maps. The meta-claim is pro-open-release, and the map gives the safety/restrictionist side its strongest, most peer-reviewed sources (tamper-resistance, Casper/Bengio/Hendrycks, Bengio risk-floor) while explicitly flagging that the pro-open side leans more on op-eds/VC advocacy — and it scores those advocacy items honestly low (the `regulatory-capture-concern` 5/4/4/6 is a model of honest weighting). The proponent rebuttals even concede "a naive citation count flatters the safety camp." This is exemplary.

**Strongest version of each side, per pillar:**
- **Marginal uplift** — Best-balanced. *For-restriction:* guardrails strippable + capability rising. *Against-restriction:* Epoch's no-clear-amateur-uplift + bottleneck-is-tacit-skill. Both steelmanned; the elicitation-attack item is a genuinely sophisticated addition that defeats a lazy pro-open talking point.
- **Irreversibility** — Correctly identified as the settled point; the live dispute (caution-at-release vs pivot-to-use-regulation) is framed precisely. NTIA + LLaMA anchor it well.
- **Democratization** — The asymmetry (diffuse benefits vs concentrated tail risk) is the crux of the whole debate and is drawn fairly; structured-access substitution is the right crux.
- **Enforceability/China** — Toner's continuum + Bengio's risk-floor genuinely contest. The "policy menu is not binary" item productively dissolves the false dichotomy.

**Weak / overstated claims:**
- The 2026 capability figures (`open-model-capability-trajectory`, `deepseek-v4-foreign-frontier`) carry specific benchmark numbers ("3,206 Codeforces," "Claude 4.5 Opus 80.9") that are speculative/post-cutoff; they should be clearly framed as illustrative trajectory, not measured fact. Their *directness to the misuse question* is correctly scored low (6).
- `tamper-resistant-safeguards-fail` pairs two papers that are partly in tension (the second critiques the first's robustness eval) as if jointly confirming the same point — direction is right but the nuance is glossed.

**Missing cruxes/evidence:**
- The **cyber-offense uplift** dimension is mentioned in premises (SWE-bench, Codeforces) but, unlike biorisk (which gets Epoch), has **no dedicated evidence item evaluating whether open models materially uplift real-world cyberattack capability** vs existing tooling — an asymmetry given how much the skeptic premise leans on cyber-coding scores.
- No item on the **empirical track record of harm to date** — i.e., that despite years of widely-available open weights (Llama, Mistral, DeepSeek), no documented mass-casualty misuse has occurred. This is the single strongest *pro-open* empirical datum and is absent; its inclusion (with the obvious "absence of evidence / capability still rising" caveat) would strengthen the for side and the marginal-uplift crux.

## 3. Prioritized Fixes (top 5)

1. **Flag the 2026 capability figures as illustrative** — explicitly mark `open-model-capability-trajectory` and `deepseek-v4-foreign-frontier` benchmark numbers (Codeforces 3,206, Claude 4.5 Opus 80.9, V4-Pro specs) as projected/unverified trajectory rather than measured fact; the structural claims survive without the precise numbers.
2. **Fix the paired-paper tension** in `tamper-resistant-safeguards-fail` — note that 2412.07097 critiques 2408.00761's durability eval; cite them with that relationship rather than as joint confirmation.
3. **Add a cyber-uplift evidence item** paralleling the biorisk one (e.g., a Cybench/CTF uplift study), since the skeptic premise leans heavily on cyber-coding capability but only biorisk gets a dedicated marginal-uplift datum.
4. **Add a "track record of harm to date" item** (no documented mass-casualty open-weight misuse despite years of availability) on the for side, with the capability-still-rising caveat — closes the strongest missing pro-open empirical point.
5. **Verify post-cutoff sources:** SSRN 5705186 (Casper/Bengio/Hendrycks), gpt-oss arXiv 2508.03153, the LessWrong elicitation roundup's underlying papers, AMI Labs / Ng-WEF claims. Where possible cite primaries over roundups/secondary sites (AICerts, LessWrong synthesis).
