## 2. Adversarial Review

**Overall balance:** Unusually good. The meta-claim is pro-settlement ("freeze on current lines… would end the killing on acceptable terms"), and the map honestly loads the strongest *anti*-settlement evidence (RUSI attrition, Minsk precedent, demonstration effect, Putin-stalling). Each pillar has a real crux. Side-coding is mostly defensible.

**Strongest version of each side, per pillar:**

- **Territorial** — *For (settle):* battlefield reversibility is genuinely near-zero on a feasible horizon; the map states this well. *Against:* the strongest skeptic point is under-developed — that a freeze ratifies an *illegal* annexation as a matter of international law (UN Charter Art. 2(4)), creating a precedent independent of battlefield facts. The map frames "rewarding aggression" rhetorically but doesn't cite the legal-norms argument (e.g., no recognition of forcible annexation since 1945 / Stimson doctrine). **Missing evidence.**
- **Security guarantees** — Both sides strong; the paradox framing is the best part of the map. *Missing crux nuance:* the proponent rebuttal asserts a "Korea model" works but the skeptic never gets the sharpest rejoinder — that the Korea armistice held because of a permanent US troop tripwire under a *formal* mutual-defense treaty, which is exactly what Russia rejects here, so the analogy may be self-refuting. This is latent in the text but not surfaced as its own evidence item.
- **Sanctions/attrition** — Well-balanced (RUSI/Kluge vs Carnegie). The "forecast has been made and missed before" point is fairly given to the proponent.
- **Ceasefire vs just-peace** — Best-balanced pillar; AEI (operational lessons) vs Atlantic Council (demonstration effect) is a genuine steelman of both. Crux correctly marked `impossible` to verify.

**Weak / overstated claims:**
- `isw-stalemate-april-2026` weighted 9/9-tier on a daily snapshot whose URL doesn't match the cited date (see §1) — **strongest single overstatement.**
- `sweden-faked-data` "5.86%" precision and "faking data" framing exceed what an intelligence-agency assertion can support; replicability self-scored 6 but arguably lower.
- Two bare/mismatched URLs (`kyivindependent.com/`, Apr-19 URL for Apr-27 claim) weaken citation integrity.

**Missing cruxes/evidence:**
- No evidence item on the **non-recognition / annexation-norm** legal dimension (territorial pillar).
- No **restraint-school primary source** (Quincy) in evidence despite being in references.
- No treatment of **Ukrainian public opinion** trajectory on territorial concessions (polls shifted 2023→2025) — directly relevant to the referendum/"can't sign it away" argument and would strengthen the *for* side empirically.
