## 2. Adversarial Review

**Overall balance:** Strong and intellectually honest. The meta-claim is pro-tariff, yet the map stacks the heaviest, most authoritative evidence (USITC, Fed, CBO, Yale, PIIE, SCOTUS, savings-investment identity) on the *against* side and repeatedly concedes in its own `reasoning` fields that the proponent reads are contestable. This is the correct epistemic posture given the actual economic consensus, but see asymmetry note below.

**Strongest version of each side, per pillar:**
- **Inflation pass-through** — *Against* is overwhelmingly strong and correctly so (incidence is well-measured). *For*'s best shot is the genuine point that pass-through is *partial* and revenue can fund offsetting cuts — fairly stated. The strongest *for* argument missing: that a one-time price-*level* shift is not *inflation* (a rate) — the map mentions "one-time price-level adjustment" in the rebuttal but doesn't give the proponent a dedicated evidence item making the level-vs-rate distinction, which is the single best pro-tariff macro rejoinder.
- **Manufacturing** — *Against* (USITC net-negative) is the empirical anchor. *For* (Compass capex/lag) is a legitimate methodological critique, well-steelmanned. Balanced.
- **Decoupling** — Best-balanced pillar; Miller (against) vs effective-rate/truce (for) genuinely contest the instrument question.
- **Deficit/alliances** — *Against* (savings-investment) is consensus; *For* (externalities of deindustrialization, extracted concessions) is given a fair hearing.

**Weak / overstated claims:**
- `hbs-burden-split` coded "for" despite 43% consumer being its largest component — defensible but the weakest side-coding in the map.
- "HCSS" mis-attribution in `yale-employment-projection` (security think tank cited for employment modeling).
- `china-effective-rate` weighted high on directness for a number that, as the map admits, "does not establish whether it slows or accelerates indigenization" — the rate is real but its *strategic* directness is lower than 7.

**Balance / asymmetry issues:**
- Across 16 evidence items, the **most authoritative sources (9-tier reliability: USITC, Fed, CBO, SCOTUS, Cavallo) are almost all coded "against"**, while the "for" side leans on an advocacy org (American Compass, indep. 5) and a private tracker. This *accurately* reflects expert consensus, but for a contested-status map it means the *for* side's strongest empirical leg — **tariff revenue funding progressive offsets that change net distribution** — is asserted in rebuttals but never given its own high-quality evidence item (e.g., a Tax Foundation/Penn Wharton revenue-recycling scenario). Adding it would make the steelman fairer.
- No evidence item on **producer-side / national-security supply-chain wins** (e.g., rare-earth or pharmaceutical-precursor reshoring) that proponents cite — the manufacturing pillar is framed almost entirely as headcount.

5. **Reconsider `hbs-burden-split` side-coding** (or split into a "for"-leaning 20%-exporter datum and an "against"-leaning 43%-consumer datum) so the largest component isn't filed under the side it least supports.
