# Argumend UX/UI Audit — Prioritized Roadmap

**Date:** 2026-06-23
**Scope:** 82 raw findings across 11 audit lenses (landing, read experience, interactive map, navigation/IA, discovery/search, content/reading, mobile, accessibility, visual design system, user-journey funnel, microcopy/tone), deduped into ~40 distinct issues.
**Verification:** Top claims spot-checked against source: nodes have **0** `dark:` classes, `text-stone-400` appears **282×**, "Map Arguments, Not Win Them" appears **0×** on-page, `getVerdictLabel` renders as a fragment `{verdict}.`, `TopicExplorer` has **0** imports, `NewsletterSignup` is **absent** from `ReadModeView`, guides have **no** markdown parser. The findings are accurate.

---

## Executive summary

Argumend is, at its core, a well-built product with an unusually strong content engine. The flagship Read-mode journey (keystone "wow" fact → 3-sentence honest case → synoptic table → steel-manned pillars → falsification cruxes → weighted evidence) is coherent and genuinely persuasive. The accessibility *foundation* is better than most products at this stage (landmarks, skip link, focus trap, ARIA roles), the mobile architecture makes the right call (no React Flow on phones), and the brand tokens are thoughtfully defined. **The problem is not the engine; it's the framing, the finishing, and the funnel.**

Five themes dominate, in priority order:

1. **The product never says what it is.** The single best line — "Map Arguments, Not Win Them" — lives only in metadata. A cold search visitor lands on a niche debate ("Consciousness in AI Systems" at 31% confidence) with no value-prop sentence, no orientation, and the interactive differentiator absent from first paint. This recurred across *four* lenses (landing, funnel, microcopy, navigation). It is the biggest first-impression failure and the cheapest cluster to fix.

2. **The funnel has no bottom.** The only working conversion (newsletter) is absent from the exact page where search traffic finishes reading. Every other engage/return affordance is disabled (Sign in, Subscribe bell, Dashboard) or orphaned (saved topics in localStorage, surfaced nowhere). Peak intent meets zero capture, and visible dead-ends actively erode trust. This is a one-day cluster with outsized ROI.

3. **Naming and visual coherence are inconsistent at the trust-deciding moment.** The second view is called "Detailed," "Open the map," and "Logic Map" with a colliding book icon; category colors are defined three times (search shows an off-brand rainbow); the verdict subhead is a grammatically broken fragment; "confidence" appears as 2–3 unbridged numbers in one viewport. Individually minor, collectively they read as unfinished on a product whose entire pitch is rigor.

4. **Two systemic defects degrade every page.** `text-stone-400` (~2.2:1 contrast) is used for ~90+ pieces of real text per page and fails WCAG AA everywhere. Dark mode is broken on the canvas (nodes have zero dark styling) and on the topic-header chips. Both are wide-blast-radius, medium-effort fixes.

5. **Built UX ships to nobody, and IA has sprawled.** A full force-directed Topic Explorer (1,106 lines) is never routed; category SEO pages are unreachable from the index; the /is hub (121 questions) has no search/filter/jump-nav; the sidebar hides 12 overlapping content sections behind a low-contrast accordion. These are the structural bets.

**Honest bottom line:** The hard part (the reasoning content and the canvas engine) is done well. What's missing is the connective tissue — telling people what they're looking at, naming things once, capturing the people who stay, and not shipping broken formatting/contrast/dark-mode on the pages search traffic actually lands on. Most of the highest-leverage fixes are S/M effort.

## Strategic bets

Higher effort or structural — fewer, deliberate investments. Sequence after the quick-wins ship.

### A. Unify the topic experience into one canonical, shareable route
- **What:** Collapse the three near-identical topic renderings (SSR read, SSR `?view=graph`, SPA `/?topic=X`) into a coherent model. Keep the topic id in the URL (`/topics/[id]?view=graph`) instead of `history.replaceState({}, "", "/")` which erases it. Standardize CTA labels/destinations so "open the map" always means the same linkable thing. Decide one canonical "deep view" destination so read/graph/detail aren't three scroll pages. Resolve the mobile "Open the map" CTA that does NOT open the map (route to `/?topic=ID` → MobileArgumentList, or relabel honestly).
- **Why:** The deep interactive state is currently unbookmarkable, unshareable, and lost on refresh — killing the shareability of the most impressive view. On mobile the headline differentiator is unreachable and the CTA lies.
- **Where:** `HomeClient.tsx:145`, `ReadModeView.tsx:516-529`, `TopicDetailView.tsx`, `TopicPageClient.tsx`
- **Effort:** L
- **Merges findings from:** funnel "two parallel experiences"; mobile #1, #3; read/microcopy naming (depends on quick-win #4)

### B. Make the legend the single source of truth for node vocabulary + colors
- **What:** Derive `MapLegend` from `VARIANT_STYLES` (or vice versa) so one canonical color+icon+label per variant drives the node header, legend, minimap, and breadcrumb dot. Fix `meta` (teal in legend, blue in variantStyles, rust in breadcrumb) to one value; reconcile "Pillar"/"question" labels that never appear in the legend.
- **Why:** The one artifact meant to teach the map ("How to Read This Map") actively misleads — users who study it then can't find what it promised, undermining the map's perceived rigor.
- **Where:** `MapLegend.tsx`, `lib/variantStyles.ts`, `NavigationPath.tsx:74-76`, node components
- **Effort:** M (structural, but contained)

### C. Consolidate category/status colors into one shared map (with dark variants)

Drop indigo/sky/violet (off-brand, not tokens) for the parchment-friendly set. Bake dark variants in. Decouple "Settled" status from "Science" category (both render emerald — green reads as a "this is true" verdict, fighting the neutral brand). Add an ESLint/CI grep for `/(amber|tangerine|yellow|indigo|violet|sky)-[0-9]/` to prevent regression.
- **Why:** Same category gets three color systems; a search-arriving user sees "indigo Technology" then "stone Technology" — the one job of category color (instant recognition) is broken, and the rainbow is off-brand. Also folds in #7's chip dark-mode so it can't drift again.

### D. Decide the fate of the orphaned Topic Explorer + lift its filters onto /topics

Either way, lift its already-built status multi-select + confidence-range filters onto the live /topics page (which has none). Make category selection URL-addressable (`?category=policy`) and link to the orphaned canonical category SEO pages.
- **Why:** Verified 0 imports — the richest discovery affordances (relationship edges, confidence-range/status filtering) ship to nobody, while /topics offers only single-category tabs. Category SEO pages are unreachable from the index (hurts both humans and internal link equity). Filtered views aren't shareable (in-memory state).

- **Effort:** M (route + lift) / L (if building the full map view)

- **Why:** 12 overlapping sections with 3 plausible destinations for "what is a strawman" inflate cognitive load; sidebar and footer use conflicting taxonomies; on first paint a search arrival sees almost no site map. This is the classic both-sides product over-categorizing itself.
- **Where:** `components/Sidebar.tsx:32-56,77,146-160`, `components/Footer.tsx:4-36`, `hooks/useSidebarState.ts:36`, new shared nav config

Once guides parse markdown (#8), enrich them with inline links to /glossary, /concepts, /fallacies, /topics ("See this in a real map") — guides are currently the weakest at converting a reader into a map-explorer (0 internal links vs blog's 96).

- **Effort:** L
- **Merges findings from:** content "no TOC/anchors," "guide dead-ends," "glossary anchors under topbar"

### G. Newsletter resilience + reconcile saved-topics
- **What:** Make `/api/newsletter` resilient: a DB outage should still return success and write to a durable fallback (log/queue/ESP webhook), or integrate a hosted ESP (Buttondown/ConvertKit) so signups never depend on your own DB. Build a client-rendered "Saved" page reading the localStorage hook (no auth) and link it; either deliver the "get pinged on score shift" promise via newsletter segmentation or change the microcopy to stop promising notifications you can't send.
- **Why:** The app "runs offline by default" yet the sole conversion calls `getDb()` with no fallback — if DATABASE_URL is ever missing in prod, every signup is silently lost. Meanwhile localStorage saves and the DB-backed dashboard are disconnected: a logged-out user can save 20 topics and never find them.
- **Where:** `app/api/newsletter/route.ts`, `components/NewsletterSignup.tsx`, `hooks/useSavedTopics.ts`, new `/saved` page
- **Effort:** M
- **Merges findings from:** funnel "newsletter silent fail," "saved topics orphaned"
- **Dependency:** sequence with quick-win #2 (capture) and #3 (dead-ends).
