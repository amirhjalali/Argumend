# Thesis Drafts

## Draft A
Argumend should become the best argument map platform by improving evidence scoring and adding more topics.

Weakness: too incremental; does not solve bridge problem.

## Draft B
Argumend should become a deliberation platform that makes disagreement productive through better protocols.

Strength: closer to mission; still vague on normative + empirical grounding.

## Draft C (Final)
Argumend should function as **public reasoning infrastructure**: a system that transforms identity-threatening disagreement into claim-structured, reciprocal inquiry by combining (1) communicative legitimacy constraints (Habermas), (2) pluralist democratic safeguards (Rawls, Arendt, Dewey, Mill, Buber, Aristotle), and (3) empirically validated bridge interventions (structured deliberation, moral reframing, wise reasoning, humility framing, challengeable evidence governance).

### Absolute Thesis
**What works is not “better arguments” alone, but protocolized reciprocity.**
A platform bridges people only when it makes users do three things in sequence:
1. Represent each other accurately (reciprocal steelman),
2. Separate claim types and test them appropriately (truth/rightness/sincerity),
3. Keep disagreement revisable through transparent evidence governance.
