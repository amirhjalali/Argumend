# Loop 06

## Inputs
- Cambridge APSR studies on deliberation and polarization.
- Nature Human Behaviour synthesis on interventions reducing affective polarization.
- Studies on moral reframing, deep canvassing, cross-partisan empathy utility, wise reasoning, intellectual humility.

## Work Performed
- Extracted intervention mechanisms with strongest observed effects.
- Identified what generalizes online vs what requires high-touch facilitation.
- Derived evidence-backed product design constraints.

## Findings
- Structured deliberation can reduce polarization and can persist beyond immediate sessions.
- Effects degrade when interventions are one-off, identity-threatening, or unmeasured.
- Moral reframing works when arguments are translated into opponent value language.
- Brief, empathic, non-coercive conversation can create durable attitude shifts in some contexts.
- Wise-reasoning and intellectual-humility framing reduce animosity and improve engagement quality.
- Multi-component intervention bundles outperform single nudges in durable outcomes.
