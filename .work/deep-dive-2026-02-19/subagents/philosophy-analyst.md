# Philosophy Analyst Output

## Habermas (primary anchor)
- Core claim: legitimacy emerges from discourse where participants can justify claims to one another under fair conditions.
- Validity claims framework: truth (facts), rightness (norms), sincerity (authentic intent), comprehensibility (clarity).
- Product implication: disagreements should be decomposed by claim-type before scoring or persuasion attempts.

## Complementary Thinkers
- Aristotle (*Rhetoric*): persuasion involves ethos/pathos/logos; platform must discipline rhetoric without dehumanizing emotion.
- J.S. Mill (*On Liberty*): viewpoint confrontation is epistemically necessary; suppression weakens truth-tracking.
- Rawls (*Political Liberalism*): public reason requires reasons others could reasonably accept across doctrines.
- Dewey (*The Public and Its Problems*): democracy as collective inquiry; institutions must make inquiry practical.
- Arendt (*The Human Condition*): plurality is irreducible; politics is appearance among distinct persons, not unanimity.
- Buber (*I and Thou*): relation quality matters; objectifying opponents (I-It) blocks understanding.

## Philosophical Design Requirement
Best-in-class argument platform must optimize for: truth-tracking + reciprocity + legitimate dissensus (not just consensus).
