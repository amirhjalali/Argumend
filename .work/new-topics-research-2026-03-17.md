## Topic 1: AI Replacing White-Collar Jobs

- **id**: `ai-white-collar-displacement`
- **title**: "AI White-Collar Job Displacement"
- **meta_claim**: "Large language models and AI agents will permanently eliminate more white-collar professional jobs than they create within the next decade, requiring fundamental restructuring of the knowledge economy."
- **status**: `contested`
- **category**: `technology`
- **questions**:
  1. "Is AI displacing jobs faster than previous technological revolutions, or does history show new jobs always emerge?" — The pace argument: automation anxieties have existed since the Luddites, but the speed and breadth of LLM capability gains since 2023 are qualitatively different from prior automation waves.
  2. "Which professions face genuine existential risk vs. augmentation?" — Distinguishing between tasks and entire professions; legal research, radiology reads, copywriting, and junior coding are already being transformed.
  3. "Can retraining programs work at the scale and speed needed?" — Historical retraining success rates have been under 30%. If displacement happens over 5 years instead of 30, the social safety net faces unprecedented strain.
- **pillars**:
  1. **Capability Acceleration** — AI capabilities are advancing exponentially, passing professional benchmarks (bar exam, medical licensing, CPA) faster than predicted.
  2. **Historical Precedent** — Every major automation wave (agriculture, manufacturing) ultimately created more jobs than it destroyed, though with painful transitions.
  3. **Economic Incentive Structure** — Corporations face intense pressure to cut labor costs; AI costs drop ~50% annually while human wages rise.
  4. **The Augmentation Thesis** — AI tools make workers more productive rather than replacing them, as happened with spreadsheets and word processors.
- **Why strategic**: This is THE conversation of 2026. Every working professional searches for "will AI take my job" variants. McKinsey, Goldman Sachs, and World Economic Forum reports generate massive coverage. Extremely high search volume, personal relevance drives sharing.
- **Trending keywords**: "AI replacing jobs 2026", "will AI take my job", "ChatGPT job displacement", "AI automation white collar", "future of work AI"

  3. **Systemic vs. Individual Framing** — Does medicalizing obesity shift focus away from food policy, urban design, and corporate accountability for the obesogenic environment?

  3. **Collective Action Problem** — Individual families cannot opt out when every other child has a smartphone; government intervention may be the only way to solve the coordination failure.
